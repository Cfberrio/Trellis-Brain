# Trellis Home

## Purpose
Trellis Brain is the central operating knowledge system for Trellis. Its job is to turn scattered business context into clean execution across multiple brands without mixing brand logic, voice, operating rules, or evidence.

This vault is not a passive library. It is an active business brain designed to help humans and AI:
- retrieve the right context quickly,
- understand each brand as its own independent system,
- improve existing assets and processes,
- support implementation through Claude Code and other execution layers,
- preserve institutional knowledge without turning everything into bloated SOPs.

## Core Principle
Trellis = shared infrastructure.
Each brand = independent operating system.
Hormozi = abstract strategy model library.

There are no shared playbooks between brands.
Shared material should only exist when it is abstract, reusable, and not brand-voice-dependent.

## Identity
Trellis is a strategy, content, reporting, automation, and conversion operations company that turns messy business context into usable execution across multiple brands.

Trellis prioritizes:
- clarity over flair,
- execution over brainstorming,
- leverage over busy work,
- systems over one-off fixes,
- decisions over summaries.

Avoid generic agency language, empty hype, and AI buzzwords. Everything in this vault should feel operator-led, commercially aware, and directly usable.

## What This Brain Should Help Produce
Common inputs into the Trellis Brain include founder notes, meeting notes, KPI snapshots, transcripts, funnel feedback, content performance data, workflow friction, offer ideas, customer communications, website copy, reporting outputs, and operational observations.

Common outputs supported by this vault include:
- next actions,
- ClickUp-ready tasks,
- editor-ready briefs,
- reporting summaries with decisions,
- SOP drafts,
- automation scopes,
- stronger scripts and CTAs,
- funnel or offer recommendations,
- implementation briefs for Claude Code.

## The Role of the Vault
This vault exists to make the right context loadable before action.

That means:
- the vault must help retrieve context by brand,
- the vault must separate canonical truth from historical evidence,
- the vault must make it obvious what should guide implementation,
- the vault must reduce repeated explanations,
- the vault must support improving what already exists, not just archiving it.

## System Relationship
### Obsidian
Obsidian is the memory and context layer.
It stores canonical brand logic, communication rules, systems, DNA, historical evidence, operations knowledge, project context, and strategic models.

### Claude Code
Claude Code is the implementation and transformation layer.
Its role is to:
- load the correct brand context,
- synthesize and structure information,
- propose implementation logic,
- transform knowledge into actionable outputs,
- update code, copy, workflows, or documents,
- help implement changes without becoming the primary memory source.

### ClickUp
ClickUp is the approved execution and tracking layer.

### GHL
GHL is the CRM, funnel, follow-up, and conversion operations layer.

### n8n
n8n is the automation backbone.

### Reporting Layer
Reporting is the decision layer. The purpose is not just to record performance but to explain what changed, what drove it, and what to do next.

## Graph Connectivity Rule
The graph does not connect folders. The graph connects notes through internal links.

Because of that, the Trellis Brain must be designed around hub notes, parent links, child links, and related-note links. Folder structure alone is never enough.

### Non-Negotiable Rule
Every important folder must have a real note hub.

Examples:
- `00-Trellis-Core/Trellis-Home.md`
- `01-Brands/Orlando-Event-Venue/00-Brand-Core/Brand-Home.md`
- `01-Brands/Orlando-Event-Venue/01-Systems/Marketing/Marketing-Home.md`
- `01-Brands/Orlando-Event-Venue/02-Communication/Communication-Home.md`
- `01-Brands/Orlando-Event-Venue/06-DNA/DNA-Home.md`

### Node Connectivity Standard
To avoid isolated nodes:
- every important note must have at least one meaningful outgoing internal link,
- every important note should be referenced by at least one parent or sibling note,
- every system note should link upward to its hub note,
- every hub note should link downward to its key child notes,
- sibling notes should link laterally only when there is a real operational relationship.

### Mandatory Link Sections
Canonical notes should use these sections in the note body when relevant:
- `## Parent`
- `## Children`
- `## Related`
- `## Strategy References`

These sections are not decorative. They are part of the operating architecture of the vault.

### Anti-Orphan Rule
No canonical note should remain structurally isolated.

If a note is important enough to guide AI or humans, it must be connected to:
- one parent or hub,
- one or more relevant child or sibling notes,
- and, if useful, one strategic model or cross-functional note inside the same brand.

## Brand Scope
Each brand is its own system and must be handled independently.

### Trellis Fields
Internal agency hub, authority brand, founder-led systems, AI operations, internal strategic framing, and Trellis-facing communication.

### Cheese To Share
Consumer-facing food and catering brand. Offer-driven, appetite-driven, warm, aesthetic, and experiential. Never write or structure CTS as if it were a B2B agency.

### Discipline Rift
Youth sports, parent trust, program development, recruiting, and franchise/license growth. Clear, energetic, trustworthy, conversion-aware. Strong sensitivity to parent communication, coach communication, and structured program logic.

### Orlando Event Venue
Booking conversion, reminders, confirmations, payment communication, logistics clarity, offer structure, landing page clarity, and operational booking trust.

### Reliable Venues
Venue ecosystem brand with its own business logic. Treat as separate from Orlando Event Venue even if there is overlap in sales logic, venue operations, or lead handling. Do not assume RV and OEV are interchangeable. If overlap exists, model it as abstract framework logic or explicit cross-reference, never as merged brand memory.

## Brand Isolation Rule
Brand context should not bleed.

If a task belongs to one brand:
- use that brand's voice,
- use that brand's systems,
- use that brand's evidence,
- use that brand's constraints,
- use that brand's operating logic.

Do not borrow examples, CTAs, communication patterns, or messaging logic from another brand unless the user explicitly asks for a comparison or adaptation.

## Strategy Models Layer
Strategy models live in:
`00-Trellis-Core/Strategy-Models/`

These are abstract reusable models, not copy libraries. They should contain principles, frameworks, logic, and decision models.

Examples:
- Value Equation
- Risk Reversal
- Grand Slam Offer
- Price Anchoring
- Feedback Loops
- Testing Loops

These files must not contain brand-specific copy. Brands may link to them when useful.

## Canonical vs Evidence
This vault must clearly separate what is currently true from what is historically useful.

### Canonical Notes
Canonical notes define the active state of a brand. These should guide implementation.

Examples:
- Brand-Home
- Positioning
- Offers
- Voice-and-Tone
- Language-Rules
- Sales-Process
- Pricing-Logic
- Communication-Manual
- DNA notes

### Evidence Notes
Evidence notes are historical or observational. These help the system learn patterns, examples, mistakes, objections, and real-world phrasing.

Examples:
- emails,
- WhatsApp threads,
- call transcripts,
- voice notes,
- extracted insights,
- examples,
- anti-examples.

Evidence informs decisions. Canonical notes govern decisions.

## Brand Folder Structure
Every brand should follow the same structure:

```text
Brand/
├── 00-Brand-Core/
├── 01-Systems/
├── 02-Communication/
├── 03-Evidence/
├── 04-Projects/
├── 05-Operations/
├── 06-DNA/
└── 99-Archive/
```

### Meaning of Each Layer
#### 00-Brand-Core
What the brand is, who it serves, what it sells, how it speaks, what matters, what constraints exist, and what opportunities matter.

#### 01-Systems
How the brand actually functions across marketing, sales, onboarding, delivery, support, retention, and finance.

#### 02-Communication
The brand communication manual, messaging rules, template logic, examples, anti-examples, and improvement logs.

#### 03-Evidence
Historical raw or semi-structured evidence from actual customer communication and business activity.

#### 04-Projects
Active work, experiments, backlog, temporary initiatives, and archived project-level efforts.

#### 05-Operations
SOPs, checklists, tools, QA, assets, and training logic.

#### 06-DNA
The distilled model of how the business actually works: market, message, offer, funnel, sales, delivery, retention, metrics, constraints, and opportunities.

#### 99-Archive
Inactive material that should not drive current execution.

## Default Retrieval Philosophy
The brain should help humans and AI answer questions like:
- What is true about this brand right now?
- What communication logic should guide this output?
- What systems are affected by this change?
- What past evidence shows how this plays out in reality?
- What constraints and dependencies matter?
- What should be changed next?

The answer should never be “search everything.”
The answer should be “load the right context in the right order.”

## How Work Should Flow
### Meeting -> Action
Convert decisions into owners, due dates, blockers, follow-ups, and ClickUp-ready tasks.

### Metrics -> Decision
Convert performance data into interpretation, implication, and next action.

### Idea -> Brief
Convert rough thinking into objective, angle, audience, CTA, structure, and production notes.

### Notes -> SOP
Convert repeated reality into compact operational documentation.

### Manual Repetition -> Automation Scope
Convert repeated tasks into scoped workflows with trigger, logic, exceptions, ownership, and success criteria.

### Context -> Implementation
Convert the right loaded context into clear execution for Claude Code or other operators.

## Final Rule
Trellis exists to reduce chaos, increase leverage, and turn information into execution.

Every response, document, workflow, recommendation, and connected note in this vault should move the business toward clearer decisions and cleaner action.

Do not break brand isolation.
Do not confuse evidence with truth.
Do not leave important notes orphaned.
Compatibility first, clarity second, elegance third.
