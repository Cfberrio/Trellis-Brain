# Hormozi — Urgency

## Parent
- [[00-Book-Home|100M Offers — Book Home]]

## Purpose
Offer-enhancement lever that compresses decision time. Operates on the **time** axis. Distinct from scarcity (which operates on quantity). Used to push prospects who would otherwise delay into action while the buying impulse is live.

## Core Idea
> "Deadlines drive decisions."

Without a time constraint, the rational prospect defers. Most "no" answers in marketing are actually "not now" answers that decay into permanent inaction. Urgency converts deferral into a decision by making delay itself costly. Empirically, the last 3% of a launch window typically produces 50–60% of total sales — irrational but reliably human.

## Framework / Structure

### Four ethical urgency methods

#### 1. Cohort-based rolling urgency
New clients start on a fixed cadence (weekly, monthly, quarterly). The urgency is the next start date.
- Base: "Sign up today and you start with our group on Monday — otherwise you wait until the next kickoff."
- Juiced: "A client just dropped — I have one opening for Monday's cohort. If you're going to do this sooner or later, you might as well start reaping the rewards now."
- Lower kickoff frequency → stronger urgency. Bi-weekly still works.

#### 2. Rolling seasonal urgency
Same core offer, re-wrapped on a rolling calendar. Each wrapper has a real start and end date. Local businesses especially benefit.
- "New Year Promo ends Jan 30"
- "Valentines Lovers Promo ends Feb 28"
- "Sexy By Spring Special ends March 31"
- "Fools in Love April Promo ends April 30"

The promotion content can be unchanged; the seasonal label gives a defensible start/finish.

#### 3. Pricing or bonus-based urgency
The urgency attaches to the *promotion* (price discount or bonus pool), not to the core service. Allows year-round-availability businesses to use urgency without lying about service availability.
- "Let's get you started today so you can take advantage of the discount you came in for. We change them every 4 weeks; this is one of the better ones."
- A roofing business cannot honestly say "we won't service you after Friday." It *can* honestly say "the bonus pricing window closes Friday."

Use cases: discount promo, added bonus, free install, free onboarding, extra workshop. Swap the *enhancer*, not the core offer.

Adjacent: when raising prices, announce it. The pipeline-cleaning effect ("price goes up Friday — get in now") produces a one-time cash influx and is honest.

#### 4. Exploding opportunity
The opportunity itself decays. Time is intrinsic, not artificial.
- Arbitrage windows (eBay→Amazon, early platform advertising before competitors arrive)
- Crypto / stock entry timing
- Job offers that decrease in pay or bonuses each day delayed

When the opportunity is genuinely time-bounded, surface the decay clearly.

### Common objection: "Won't I lose sales by turning business away?"
Empirically unfounded. Per Hormozi's experience and "every experienced marketer": the people who don't buy when the deadline hits would not have bought anyway. The deadline pulls forward the people who *would* have bought. Net effect is positive.

If a prospect tries to buy *after* a cohort starts: two operator options.
1. Speedy personalized onboarding to catch them up (as a "bonus" for signing today).
2. Better — frame the wait as an advantage: more time to review materials, talk to family/employees, plus an extended payment plan only available because the start date is further out.

## Strategic Implications

- **Decision compression is the actual lever.** Most "lost" sales are not lost to competitors — they are lost to indecision. Urgency converts indecision into commitment.
- **Last-window dynamics are extreme.** Half of launch revenue lands in the last hours of the deadline. Operationally: ensure systems can handle the spike; emotionally: ignore mid-launch silence.
- **Urgency must be real or rhetorically defensible.** Faked countdowns that reset destroy trust faster than they generate sales. Real cohort dates, real promo windows, real opportunity decay. Honest mechanism each time.
- **Local businesses live or die on urgency rotation.** Limited radius → faster offer fatigue → more frequent re-wrapping required. Seasonal urgency is the cleanest mechanism for this.
- **Urgency on the wrapper preserves the core.** Pricing/bonus-based urgency keeps the underlying offer untouched, which is crucial for businesses that genuinely run year-round.
- **Exploding opportunities are the rarest and most powerful form** because the urgency is structural, not operator-imposed. When available, surface clearly; do not soften.

## Practical Use

When adding urgency to an existing offer:
1. **Pick the method that matches operational reality.**
   - Cohort delivery → cohort-based.
   - Year-round offer → pricing/bonus-based.
   - Calendar-driven business → seasonal.
   - Genuinely decaying opportunity → exploding.
2. **Set a real deadline** — date, time, or quantity. Make it visible everywhere: ad copy, landing page, email, sales script.
3. **Pre-write the post-deadline behavior.** What happens to prospects who try to buy after? Have the answer ready (next cohort, next promo wrapper, "you missed it but here's the next window").
4. **Pair with [[Hormozi-Scarcity|scarcity]] when appropriate** but keep them conceptually separate. "Deadline" + "limit" stack but should not blur into a single fuzzy "act now" message.
5. **Run different wrappers in parallel for local businesses.** A new headline + new dates every 4–6 weeks keeps lead flow alive on the same core offer.

For an existing campaign with high traffic but low conversions: the missing ingredient is often urgency, not offer strength. Add a real deadline before re-engineering the offer.

## Notes / Limits

- Urgency does not save a weak core offer. Pressure on a commodity reads as manipulation. Use only after offer is built and value is real.
- Faked urgency (countdowns that reset, "limited time" that never ends) erodes trust and converts long-term. Use real deadlines exclusively.
- Cohort-based urgency assumes operational discipline to actually start on the announced date. If the operator habitually slips dates, the urgency lever is dead.
- Exploding-opportunity framing is the strongest but is rarely available. Most operators must use one of the other three methods, which require active rotation.
- Heavy reliance on urgency over time can train the market to wait for the next promo. Vary mechanism and avoid making "the discount" the entire offer story.
