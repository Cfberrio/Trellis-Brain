# Hormozi — Hook Library (121 Best)

## Parent

## Purpose
Preserved swipe file of the **121 hooks** Alex Hormozi documents as his and Leila Hormozi's top-performing hooks across ads, long-form content, IG, email, and Twitter for the year preceding the playbook. Sorted by views (content) and performance (ads/email) in the source. Captured here verbatim, organized by channel, as a retrieval-ready reference for hook generation. This is the primary 70%-bucket input the [[Hormozi-Hook-70-20-10-Process|70-20-10 Process]] consumes.

## Core Idea
> "These playbooks aren't museum pieces. They're meant to be used."

Hormozi's logged top-121 hooks, by channel, in order. Used directly (as 70%-bucket inputs), as variant seeds (translated across the [[Hormozi-Hook-Types-Palette|Types Palette]] formats), or as classification training data ("which format does each winner live in?").

## Strategic Implications

- **Channel matters more than format.** The library's structure makes the cross-channel pattern visible: Ads lean heavily on labels, conditionals, and direct commands; long-form Content opens with credibility statements and narratives; IG dominates with provocative statements and story openers; Email subject lines lean on curiosity, brackets, and conditionals; Twitter outliers are aphorisms. Borrowing a winning format across channels without translation is the most common hook-misfire.
- **Personality-anchored hooks dominate Content and IG.** Hooks 29 ($46.2M sale, 17M/mo), 31 (1-in-250 businesses), 36 (4 businesses cracked 10M in a row), 45 (3 companies bought/sold), 47 ($100M by 29) — all credibility-anchored openers. The pattern: state a verifiable result, then promise the lesson behind it. Operators without that credibility to draw on need to substitute either a borrowed credibility (case study, customer name) or a sharper provocation/story open.
- **Curiosity-loop subject lines win in email.** Most of the email winners (94, 95, 97, 100, 101, 105, 108, 109) are open-loop micro-questions that don't reveal value — they imply that something is being withheld. This is structurally different from ad-line winners, which usually state value upfront.
- **Twitter outliers are mostly aphorism + reveal.** All eleven Twitter winners follow a structure: short declarative claim + line break + sharper line that recontextualizes. None are questions. None are commands. The format is uniform, which is itself the lesson — Twitter's hook surface is constrained by the platform.
- **Use the library as the 70%-bucket starting point.** Per [[Hormozi-Hook-70-20-10-Process|70-20-10]], 70% of new batch hooks should be drawn from proven winners. Until the operator has a personal swipe file of equal volume, this library is the substitute 70% bucket.

## Practical Use

**As a swipe file:**
- When writing hooks for a channel, open the section matching the channel. Pick the 5–10 hooks most structurally adjacent to the asset's intent. Use them directly (with the operator's own audience and value promise substituted), or translate them across the [[Hormozi-Hook-Types-Palette|Types Palette]] formats.

**As classification training:**
- Take any hook from the library. Identify which Types Palette format it lives in (label / question / conditional / command / statement / list / narrative / exclamation). After classifying 20–30 hooks, the operator's intuition for format-fit improves dramatically.

**As a portfolio-balance check:**
- The channel-composition table reveals where Hormozi himself invests the most hook-engineering volume (IG-Alex: 33 hooks). For an operator deciding how to split hook-writing effort across channels, the source-distribution proportions are a defensible starting allocation.

**As content seeds:**
- Each content/IG hook is a video premise. Pull 5 unused hooks per week, write the 30-second video that earns the hook, publish.

## Notes / Limits

- **Verbatim preservation.** Hooks are quoted as written in the source playbook, including Hormozi's stylistic choices (censored expletives, ellipses, casing, em-dashes). Do not "clean up" — the surface form is part of what worked.
- **Performance-relative, not absolute.** These are top-121 *for Hormozi's audiences and brands* (Acquisition.com, Skool, fitness/business creators) over a single year window. They are starting templates, not universally optimal hooks. Translation to a different audience is mandatory.
- **Some hooks are credibility-conditional.** A hook like *"I sold 9 companies"* (29) or *"$100M net worth by 29"* (47) doesn't transfer to an operator without that profile. Substitute equivalent legitimate proof or change format.
- **Email winners are subject lines, not full hooks.** The hook in email is the subject + preview text combination; this library captures only the subject side. Pair with a strong preview line in production.
