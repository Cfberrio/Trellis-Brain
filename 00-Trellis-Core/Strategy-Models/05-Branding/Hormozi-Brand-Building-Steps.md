# Hormozi — Brand Building Steps

## Parent
- [[00-Book-Home|Branding — Book Home]]

## Purpose
The construction method for a deliberate brand. Sequential 4-step process to start a brand from zero, grow an existing one, or pivot a misaligned one. Pairs with [[Hormozi-Branding-Definition|Branding Definition]] (what brand *is*) and [[Hormozi-Brand-Economics|Brand Economics]] (why this is worth doing) to provide the operational path.

## Core Idea
Building a brand reduces to four decisions in order: *who are we serving, what do they already like, how do we associate ourselves with those things, and how do we adjust the mix for maximum profit.* Skipping or scrambling the order produces accidental branding — the mode most businesses operate in by default. The first three steps stand up a brand; the fourth is a continuous portfolio operation, expanded in [[Hormozi-Brand-Bouquet|Brand Bouquet]].

## Framework / Structure

### The 4 steps

| Step | Decision | Output |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Figure out who the ideal customer is | A defined avatar that meets four selection criteria |
| 2 | Figure out what they like | A list of desires, problems, content preferences, role models |
| 3 | Associate the brand with those things | Content, products, partnerships, public appearances aligned to step 2 |
| 4 | Optimize associations for maximum profit | Continuous pruning and expansion of the pairing portfolio (see [[Hormozi-Brand-Bouquet\|Brand Bouquet]]) |

### Step 1 — Avatar selection: GMEP criteria

An ideal-customer market must satisfy four conditions. The mnemonic carried in this note is **GMEP** (Growing, Money, Easy-to-target, Pain) — a local convention to keep the criteria addressable.

| Criterion | Question | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Growing** | Is the audience expanding, flat, or shrinking? | Even if you stay the same size, a growing audience grows the business. A shrinking one taxes every other lever. |
| **Money** | Can they afford what you intend to charge? | Premium pricing (see [[Hormozi-Brand-Economics\|Brand Economics]]) requires customers with the cash to pay it. |
| **Easy to target** | Can you place advertising in front of them with little effort? | Customers who exist but cannot be reached are not customers. |
| **Pain** | Do they desperately want what you sell? | Pain plus competition is better than no pain. Existing buying behavior is the strongest signal a market is worth entering. |

This is an operational specialization of [[../01-100M-Offers/Hormozi-Starving-Crowd|Starving Crowd]]. Where Starving Crowd is the upstream market gate, GMEP is the four-question filter applied when scoping a brand's avatar.

Worked example from the source: a business-law firm choosing assisted-living center owners.
- Growing — aging population, expanding category. ✓
- Money — well-funded operators. ✓
- Easy to find — direct ad targeting available. ✓
- Pain — heavy regulatory, employment, and tax exposure. ✓

The avatar is too narrow only if it cannot support the revenue target; "too broad" is the more common mistake and produces accidental brands.

### Step 2 — Desire and problem inventory

For the chosen avatar, list:
- What they want (outcomes, status markers, time/money goals).
- What they fight (recurring problems, costs, threats).
- What they already consume (content, products, role models, environments).

The assisted-living example produced eight items spanning revenue, cost, hiring, retention, lawsuits, taxes, and exit value — an inventory broad enough to seed years of pairings.

This step is a research operation, not a creative one. The point is to find pairings that already work in the audience's mind — not to invent new desires. (See [[../01-100M-Offers/Hormozi-Value-Equation|Value Equation]] note: desire pre-exists; it is channeled, not created.)

### Step 3 — Association mechanics

Three sub-actions, in order:
1. **Make content geared toward the audience.** Videos, blogs, articles, booklets, PDFs — formatted around their problems and outcomes from step 2.
2. **Distribute it.** Push the content into channels where the avatar already is. Distribution is the advertising layer (reach) — see [[Hormozi-Brand-Measurement|Brand Measurement]].
3. **Iterate based on response.** Strengthen what produces toward-direction behavior; cut what does not.

Three categories of association compound over time, in increasing order of trust impact:
- **What you say** — owned media, ads, outreach. Lowest authority, highest control.
- **What other people say** — affiliates, customers, third parties. Higher authority.
- **What they experience themselves** — the product. Highest authority. (See [[Hormozi-Levels-Of-Authority|Levels of Authority]] for the cycle.)

Step 3 deliberately starts at "what you say" because it is the only layer the operator controls directly. The other two are earned by execution on this one.

### Step 4 — Optimization (pointer)

Step 4 is the continuous loop of expanding and pruning pairings to maximize profit, including the five expansion vectors (up / down / adjacent / broader / narrower), pivot management, and mistake recovery. Treated as its own framework: [[Hormozi-Brand-Bouquet|Brand Bouquet]].

### One-page checklist (appendix)

The book closes with a checklist version of this method. Captured here as the operational form of the same content:

- [ ] Figure out who your ideal avatar is (Growing, In Pain, Can Afford, Easy to find).
- [ ] Figure out what they like.
- [ ] Associate yourself with the stuff they like through:
  - [ ] Making content they like.
  - [ ] Making products they like (free and paid).
  - [ ] Appearing next to people they like.
- [ ] Grow influence and direction by associating with bigger things the largest group of potential customers like — choose one of: Up market, Down market, Broader, Narrower, Adjacent.
- [ ] Grow reach further by doing more advertising.
- [ ] Match products to promises — close the virtuous cycle (you say → others say → they say).
- [ ] When mistakes happen, double down on what they like rather than retreat.
- [ ] Wait five years and do not give up.

## Strategic Implications

- **Order is non-negotiable.** Choosing pairings before defining the avatar produces brands that drift toward whichever audience the content accidentally attracts. Hormozi's own example: when his content widened, he attracted "wantrepreneurs" instead of business owners — and had to deliberately re-narrow.
- **GMEP is a filter, not a generator.** The criteria reject bad avatars; they do not invent good ones. The choice of which industry / niche to serve is upstream and follows category and capability fit.
- **Step 2 produces the asset library.** A complete desire/problem/role-model inventory is the input to every content brief, ad, and partnership decision in step 3. Treat it as a living document, not a one-time exercise.
- **Distribution and pairing are different problems.** Step 3a–b is advertising (reach). Step 3c (iteration) is branding (influence × direction). Most businesses skip 3c, which is why their brand is accidental.
- **Step 4 begins immediately, not later.** As soon as step 3 starts producing data, optimization begins. The 4 steps are not a waterfall; steps 1–3 establish the position, step 4 manages it forever.

## Practical Use

To run the method on a business in any state:

1. **Where are you on the 4 steps?** Honest read: most operators are doing step 3 (making content) without ever having completed step 1 (named the avatar) or step 2 (catalogued what they like).
2. **Run step 1 against GMEP.** Score the current avatar on Growing / Money / Easy to find / Pain. Any single failure forces a re-scope.
3. **Audit step 2.** Produce the desires/problems/role-models inventory for the chosen avatar. If your team cannot list 8–15 items in an hour, you do not yet know the audience.
4. **Map step 3 outputs against the inventory.** For every piece of content, every ad, every partnership in the last 90 days, identify which step-2 item it pairs with. Items with zero coverage are gaps; items over-covered may indicate accidental narrowing.
5. **Set step 4 cadence.** Monthly review of audience response (engagement, sales, qualitative comments) drives prune/expand decisions per [[Hormozi-Brand-Bouquet|Brand Bouquet]].

## Notes / Limits

- The method assumes an addressable audience exists. Categories with no growing, paying, reachable, in-pain segment cannot be branded into existence; they need a different business, not a different brand.
- The four GMEP criteria are necessary but not sufficient. They prevent obvious avatar mistakes; they do not by themselves identify the sharpest sub-niche inside a qualifying market.
- Hormozi's narrowing experience (business owners vs. wantrepreneurs) shows that audience drift can be invisible until revenue mix shifts. Step 4 needs revenue-side instrumentation, not just engagement metrics.
