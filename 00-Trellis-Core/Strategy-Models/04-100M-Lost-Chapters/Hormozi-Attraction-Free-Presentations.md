# Hormozi — Attraction: Free Presentations

## Parent
- [[00-Book-Home|100M Lost Chapters — Book Home]]

## Purpose
A specific attraction-stage mechanic: free education-with-offer that compresses trust-building into a fixed window. Used to convert cold traffic into buyers for complex or expensive offers where a single ad doesn't supply enough information for a purchase decision.

## Core Idea
Buyers need a minimum amount of information before they buy; the more expensive or complex the offer, the more they need. Presentations bridge this information gap because they (1) capture attention, (2) hold it long enough to deliver the explanation, and (3) make the offer at the moment of peak motivation. The mechanism is reverse-engineered trust-building: forced consumption of value-in-advance content turns strangers into qualified prospects who *want* to buy.

Hormozi's framing: *"The bigger the plane, the longer the runway."* A $67 product takes a 60-second ad; a $100,000 consulting service takes a two-day workshop.

## Framework / Structure

### The Three Reasons Presentations Work
1. **Captive audience.** Showing up signals attention. Easier to deliver depth than in noisy advertising environments.
2. **Time investment expectation.** Attendees know presentations require time and stay for it.
3. **Peak motivation timing.** A good presentation clarifies the problem, the post-solution future, the speed/ease of getting there, social proof from peers, and resolves common concerns. The offer lands when motivation is highest.

### Three Core Limiting Beliefs
Every effective presentation works through these three beliefs. More presentations / longer presentations let you go deeper into each.

**1. Results must come easy.**
- The product is easy to use; not frustrating or overwhelming.
- The product/service lives up to claims; the universe is fair.
- It was unfair before because the prospect had an information disadvantage; now they don't.
- The universe will bend to their will; they will get status because they deserve it.

**2. Results must be the best possible.**
- Past failures aren't the prospect's fault; someone or something else is to blame.
- Failing again would feel horrible — but they won't fail, which proves the product is good.

**3. Results must lead to attention and approval (status).**
- The product positions the prospect above others. External forces will bend to their will.
- People not behaving the way the prospect wanted in the past wasn't their fault.
- People will respond now, which is why the product works.

### Format Library (with benchmarks)

**Free XYZ Dinner / Free Niche Dinner (e.g., Free Diabetic Dinner, Free Neuropathy Dinner).**
- Free dinner; 60–90 minute presentation during the meal; lower offer at the end (covers cost of bringing everyone); set individual appointments for higher-ticket offer.
- *Benchmark:* convert ~25% of room to lower offer; of those, ~33% to higher offer. 100-person room → ~25 take lower → ~8 take higher.

**Free 90-minute Masterclass.**
- 90-minute presentation, remote, structured to break core beliefs around the prospect's goal.
- *Benchmark:* 10% of attendees take the offer when presented.

**Free 5-Day Challenge.**
- Four live 60–90 minute presentations Mon–Thu; offer lands Friday. Each day attacks one core limiting belief plus delivers something usable that day.
- *Benchmark:* 2–5% of sign-ups convert.
- *Industry-specific challenge ideas:* Entrepreneurship → "Find Your Niche." Real estate consulting → "Find Your First Real Estate Deal." Business flipping → "Buy a Business with $0 Down." Sales consulting → "Make Your First High-Ticket Sale." Weight loss → "Plateau Buster." Addiction → "5 Days to Freedom." Pain → "5 Day Pain Release." Life coach → "5 Days to Get Unstuck."

**Free 3-Day Virtual Summit.**
- Multi-speaker, three-day deep dive; pitch at end of Day 2, repitch on Day 3, second presentation of Day 3 + ending inspiration. Days of week don't matter.
- Best fit for the highest-ticket offers because it stacks the most consumable content.
- *Benchmark:* 10% of final-day attendees convert.

**Free Strategy Call.**
- Step one of a two-step sale. Frame as a value-add call that doubles as pre-qualification. Triage on **BANT** — Budget, Authority, Need, Timing. Provide value by listening + identifying needs + offering solutions; if they want implementation help, make the offer.
- *Benchmark:* 25% conversion of those who make it to the second call.

### The Cold-Traffic Logic
Hormozi's Gym Launch finding (referenced from [[Hormozi-Avatar-Selection|Avatar Selection]]): 78% of top customers consumed at least two long-form pieces before buying. Reverse-engineered into the sales process → forced two long-form consumptions before any close. Result: total strangers → buyers.

Rule of thumb to sell by: **the more time they spend with you, the more money they'll spend with you.**

## Strategic Implications

- **Presentation length scales with price + audience temperature.** Cold audience + high ticket = longer runway. Warm audience + low ticket = 60-second ad. Mismatching length to price/temperature is the most common conversion failure for complex offers.
- **The three beliefs are universal.** Regardless of vertical, presentations that don't address ease, possibility, and status under-convert. The longer the format, the more depth available on each belief.
- **Format selection follows ticket and audience.** Strategy call for warm leads / high-ticket. Masterclass for cold-to-warm / mid-ticket. Challenge for cold + lower trust. Summit for highest-ticket cold conversion.
- **Follow-up is part of the presentation.** Email, SMS, and value-add content between sessions raise show rates and conversion. Anything that increases consumption increases sales.
- **Worst-case is goodwill.** In challenges and summits, value is delivered whether the prospect buys or not — losses convert to brand goodwill rather than dead spend.

## Practical Use

To pick a format:

1. **Estimate audience temperature** (cold → warm).
2. **Estimate offer ticket size** ($-$$$$).
3. **Map to format:**
   - Warm + medium → masterclass.
   - Cold + low/medium → 5-day challenge.
   - Cold + high → 3-day summit.
   - Warm + qualified pipeline → strategy call.
   - Local + low/medium → niche dinner.

To structure any presentation:
1. Open by clarifying the problem and the post-solution future.
2. Hit each of the three core beliefs (ease, possibility, status). Allocate more depth to the belief most blocking *your* avatar.
3. Show social proof — people like the audience who succeeded.
4. Resolve the most common purchase concerns.
5. Make the offer at peak motivation (last 10–20% of presentation).
6. Engineer follow-up: SMS reminders, email drips, post-event value content. Boost show rate and conversion both.

To benchmark:
- Use the format-specific conversion benchmarks above as initial targets, not commitments.
- If actual conversion is below benchmark by 50%+, suspect: belief misallocation (wrong belief in focus), wrong format for ticket/temperature, or weak offer.

## Notes / Limits
- Benchmarks are Hormozi's experience numbers, not industry universals. Vertical, audience quality, and presenter skill all shift them.
- Free Strategy Call references a "two-step sales process" that Hormozi flagged as a future book topic. Treat the BANT triage as the load-bearing piece here.
- Author note: chapter cut from `$100M Money Models` because it "got too close to pitching and selling." Some of the structural depth on presentation construction is implicit; the three-beliefs framework is the durable distillation.
- Long-form presentation depends on the presenter's ability to entertain + deliver value + break beliefs simultaneously. Without that skill, format alone won't close.
