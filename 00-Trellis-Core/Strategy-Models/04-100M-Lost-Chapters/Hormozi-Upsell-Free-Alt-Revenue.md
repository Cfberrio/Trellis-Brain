# Hormozi — Upsell: Free with Alternate Revenue Stream

## Parent
- [[00-Book-Home|100M Lost Chapters — Book Home]]

## Purpose
A specific upsell mechanic: give core service A free, monetize via complementary product or service B. Two structural variants (Paired and Independent). Used to convert "no" prospects into customers, generate referrals, capture cash from the people who otherwise leave empty-handed, and run a profitable acquisition channel even with a fully free front end. Hormozi calls the source pattern *"the no sale sale."*

## Core Idea
Prospects want the same problem solved in different ways. Offering one path traps the conversion to that single path. Offering a free alternate path with monetization on a complementary product captures revenue from prospects who reject the primary offer — and frequently produces *higher* per-customer revenue than the rejected primary. The structural insight: people who said no to your gym membership often spend more on supplements than the gym members do. Don't escort them out — sell them what they will buy.

Mechanism: **Thing A is free. Thing B is the revenue.** Either as a paired requirement (B unlocks/protects A) or an independent recommendation (B is necessary for A's success).

## Framework / Structure

### Two Variants

**Paired Upsell.**
Free A, in exchange for buying B. B is structurally tied to A.
- Storage: free month of storage → must buy proprietary lock that only fits the unit's doors.
- Coaching + software: free coaching for life, conditional on continued software subscription.

**Independent Upsell.**
Free A, with strong recommendation to buy B for A to work well. B is contextually tied to A.
- Weight loss: free 28-day program → at nutrition orientation, $400 supplement package "to maximize results."
- Physical therapy: 4 free treatments → orthotics, bands, braces, oils, athletic tape.
- Free real-estate book → upsells: shipping cost, audio version, deal contract templates, deal-finding training, financing-with-no-money training. Each upsell is a *necessary requisite* for using the book's strategies.

### Three Structural Roles
This mechanic can sit in three places in a money model:
1. **Downsell after primary refusal.** The "no sale sale" pattern — give the rejected prospect the free version, schedule a follow-up appointment, sell the complementary product at that appointment.
2. **Primary front-end attraction.** Lead with the free thing, monetize via the alternate stream. Hormozi: gym owner moved this from a downsell to the front end and it worked as a standalone acquisition channel.
3. **Free trial + alternate revenue + back-end continuity.** Combined: free service trial converts a card on file (Free Trial + Penalty mechanic), supplements upsell at the trial onset, continuity bills downstream. Captures multiple streams in parallel.

### Effectiveness Drivers
1. **Prospect's perception of B as essential/required.** Storage lock is the canonical example — without B, A doesn't work. The more essential B feels, the higher the take rate.
2. **Frictionless upsell.** Card on file at A's signup, B is one-click. Hormozi: 90%+ take rates achievable on these upsells when friction is removed.

### Worked Liquidation Math
Even at $0 on the free thing:
- 80% of free-takers take a $300 upsell at 80% margins.
- $300 × 80% × 80% = **$192 of GP per free thing given away** — and that's just the first upsell. There will be (and should be) others.

### Discount Wrapper Variant
Works with a discount, unlike Pick Your Price or Freemium. Real example: 200+ weight-loss-clinic chain offered $5/month service for a year, *contingent* on the client purchasing the chain's supplements/bars/shakes/meal program. Guarantee was also contingent on continued usage. Doctors and nurses staffed; service was effectively given away because consumable margins covered everything. Insight: people pay more readily for tangible goods than for white-coat professional service.

### "No Upsell = No Stick" Rule
**The single greatest predictor of back-end conversion is whether the customer takes the first upsell.** A prospect who buys A but rejects B is unlikely to stay on continuity. The upsell isn't optional — it's the gating signal for everything downstream. Treat the upsell sale as a must-have, not nice-to-have.

### Internal-Audience Use
Run this play to existing audiences as well: encourage them to bring friends for the free thing. Free + low-friction = high attendance. Each attendee is upsell-eligible.

Comparison logic: 100 prospects + sell 30 a $99 thing vs 100 prospects + 100 take a free thing → 80 take a $300 upsell. The latter produces more customers, more cash, and more referrals.

## Strategic Implications

- **"No" becomes revenue, not loss.** This is the structural answer to the standard funnel problem of losing 100% of revenue from rejected prospects. Done well, near-100% of front-end "nos" still produce revenue.
- **Operational requirement: B has high margin.** The model requires the alternate-revenue product to absorb both A's fulfillment cost and A's acquisition cost. Low-margin Bs break the math.
- **Multi-step sales process is mandatory.** Cannot run on a single page. The sequence is: opt-in to free → first session/visit → upsell B at that session. Skipping the in-person/in-call upsell removes the primary monetization mechanism.
- **Cold traffic adjustment.** With cold prospects, close a Free Trial + credit card on the first transaction, then upsell product at the second visit. Otherwise no-show rate kills the model.
- **The first upsell is the long-term-value gate.** A take on the upsell predicts retention. A reject predicts churn. Sales focus belongs on the upsell, not the front-end.
- **Tangible-goods preference.** Real-world insight from the weight-loss-clinic example: customers spend on tangible products more readily than on professional services. Where possible, build a tangible-goods component into a service business.

## Practical Use

When to use:
- The business has a high-margin product or service complementary to a low-margin core.
- Sales process is one-on-one (visit, call, in-person event).
- Front-end "no" rate is materially leaving revenue on the table.

Build sequence:
1. **Pick A and B.** A is the free / cheap front end (low operational cost, deliverable broadly). B is the monetization (high margin, frictionless to add).
2. **Decide variant.** Paired (B structurally required) or Independent (B recommended for A's success).
3. **Choose role.** Front-end attraction / downsell after primary / combined with free trial.
4. **Design the upsell touchpoint.** Where exactly does B get sold? Visit? Orientation? Follow-up call? This must be a committed appointment, not a hope.
5. **Card on file at A's signup.** Removes friction at B's close. ~90%+ take rates depend on this.
6. **Stack downstream.** B's purchase signals retention. Customers who take B should be moved into continuity offers (see [[Hormozi-Continuity-Lifetime-Upgrades|Lifetime Upgrades]] / [[Hormozi-Continuity-Lifetime-Discounts|Lifetime Discounts]]).
7. **Monitor "no upsell" rate.** Customers who buy A but reject B are flagged for likely churn — adjust sales process or product fit.

## Notes / Limits
- 80% / $192 numbers are illustrative; actual conversion depends on B's perceived essentialness and sales execution.
- Author note: chapter cut from `$100M Money Models` because Hormozi *"didn't think enough businesses would be able to use it."* Eligibility is real — without a high-margin alternate stream, this doesn't work.
- For purely digital businesses, the upsell must be on a second touchpoint (orientation video, follow-up email sequence with offer). Real-time in-person upsells aren't possible; take rates drop accordingly.
- The "no sale sale" naming is from a gym-owner anecdote. Source spelling preserved.
- Discount-wrapper variant requires margin discipline — the $5/mo service example only worked because consumables carried the full P&L.
