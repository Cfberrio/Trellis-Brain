# Hormozi — Fast Cash Play

## Parent
- [[00-Book-Home|Fast Cash — Book Home]]

## Purpose
Umbrella concept of the *Fast Cash* playbook. Defines what a Fast Cash Play is, who it goes to, what to sell, how to price it, how many to release, and how to operate the sale. The mechanism converts existing customer demand into immediate cash inside a ≤7-day window, without new lead acquisition cost. Sets the structural target the Promo Sequence and Cadence notes operationalize.

## Core Idea
> "Revenue is vanity. Profit is sanity. Cash is reality."
>
> Fast Cash Plays are limited-time offers, advertised to your warmest audiences, designed to make the most money in the shortest period possible from the people most likely to buy.

A Fast Cash Play is a structurally complete offer with four enforced properties:
1. **Scarcity** — capped number of buyers (limited spots).
2. **Urgency** — capped time window (typically 7 days).
3. **Exclusivity** — high-touch / unscalable value the standard offer does not include.
4. **Big-ticket pricing** — 10×–50× the operator's normal cart value.

Sent only to **warmest audiences** (existing customers, previous customers in good standing, engaged leads). The premise: these audiences are already qualified and already paying for permission-to-contact, so the marginal acquisition cost of the sale is near zero — and the bigger the price, the better the margin.

## Framework / Structure

### Audience: who the play is sent to
- **Existing customers** (highest priority).
- **Previous customers in good standing**.
- **Engaged leads** with explicit permission to be contacted about your products.

Explicitly excluded:
- **Cooled-off leads** — anyone who has not received a message from the operator in 6+ months. Warm them up *for the next play*; do not include in this one.
- **Cold prospects / public ads** — Fast Cash is *not* a cold-traffic offer.

This is what separates Fast Cash from a standard nurture or product launch: the entire mechanism depends on the audience being already-warm and already-trusting.

### What to sell — unscalable premium value
Compose the offer from "unscalable" components — the things operators normally cut for being non-leveraged. In a Fast Cash play those *are* the value. Hormozi's lever palette is its own canonical note: see [[Hormozi-Unscalable-Value-Levers|Unscalable Value Levers]].

Required composition shape:
- **1 to 3 core components** (the spine of the offer).
- **3 bonuses** held in reserve, dripped one at a time across the campaign to expand the perceived price-to-value gap on each touch.
- All bonuses are honored for **all buyers regardless of when in the sequence they bought** — early buyers receive later-revealed bonuses too. The drip is a marketing mechanism, not a fairness gate.

Construction tool: use [[../01-100M-Offers/Hormozi-Offer-Creation|Offer Creation Process Part I & II]] from `$100M Offers` to build the body. Do not strip "unscalable" components — those components are what justify the price.

### How many to sell — the cap
- Default cap: **5–10% of the customer base**, sized so it will *absolutely* sell out.
- Operator with 100 customers → cap 5–10.
- Operator with 1,000+ customers → fulfillment capacity will likely cap before the percentage does. Take the lower number.
- Tiny customer base → personal/private outreach to all, expecting a few to bite.

The objective is not maximum unit volume. It is **selling out fast** — fast sell-out makes the next Fast Cash Play more compelling and shortens the window in subsequent runs.

### What to charge — premium pricing
- Take the operator's average cart value or transaction size.
- **Add a zero (10×). To go aggressive, multiply by 5 again (50×).**
- Range: 10×–50× current price.
- Rule: the price must shock; the only thing that should shock the buyer as much as the price is the value that backs it.
- Pricing is not relative to the operator's other products. It is set to **select for buyers with purchasing power**.

Premium pricing posture context: [[../01-100M-Offers/Hormozi-Pricing-Power|Pricing Power]].

### How to sell — two delivery patterns
The sale mechanism shapes the campaign sequence. See [[Hormozi-Fast-Cash-Promo-Sequence|Fast Cash Promo Sequence]] for the full sequences.

**Pattern A — Push to Consult (preferred for high-ticket).**
A 1-on-1 consultation precedes purchase. Cart opens day one; prospects book consults distributed across the 7-day window so a small sales team (often the operator alone) can meet every buyer. Limited spots + first-come-first-serve still apply. Personal sale of a personal product converts higher.

**Pattern B — Push to Automated Checkout.**
For audiences too large for 1-on-1, or strong brands that close without a consult. Build tension in advance, open the cart to everyone simultaneously, run as a stampede. First-come-first-serve.

**Low-tech fallback.** If the operator already holds card-on-file, "reply 'I'm in'" → charge card with permission. If no card on file, immediate phone call to collect on response.

### Promotion window
- Default: **≤7 days** *or* until spots run out, whichever is sooner.
- Longer than 7 days erodes urgency. Shorter than 7 days leaves sales on the table for consult flows that need scheduling room.

### Lever stack
A Fast Cash Play deliberately stacks the offer-level levers from `$100M Offers`:
- [[../01-100M-Offers/Hormozi-Scarcity|Scarcity]] — supply cap on spots.
- [[../01-100M-Offers/Hormozi-Urgency|Urgency]] — 7-day deadline.
- [[../01-100M-Offers/Hormozi-Bonuses|Bonuses]] — 3-bonus reserve dripped across the sequence.
- [[../01-100M-Offers/Hormozi-Grand-Slam-Offer|GSO architecture]] — the offer body is GSO-shaped.

The Fast Cash mechanism is not a separate offer theory. It is the GSO architecture, time-boxed and audience-restricted to a warm list.

## Strategic Implications

- **The warm list is the asset.** A Fast Cash Play turns an existing customer base into an on-demand cash mechanism. The same play, run cold, fails. The list — not the offer — is the leverage point.
- **Cash optionality is structural insurance.** Every business hits unforeseen cash drains (broken equipment, legal fees, tax bills, payroll-window mismatches). A Fast Cash Play is the operator's standing "get out of jail free" mechanism. Hormozi treats this as a permanent capability, not a one-shot tactic.
- **Premium price is not a bonus, it's the design.** A Fast Cash Play priced at 2× normal does not work — it gets compared to the standard offer and read as a discount-adjacent. 10×–50× breaks comparability and selects for the segment with purchasing power. Underpricing the play kills the mechanism.
- **The cap is the offer.** Removing the cap (selling unlimited units) breaks scarcity, breaks the high-touch promise, and collapses pricing power. Selling out fast is the structural feature, not a side-effect.
- **Bonus drip > one-shot pitch.** Holding bonuses in reserve and revealing them across the sequence weaponizes the sequence — each touch carries new value, not a re-statement of yesterday's pitch. Listed all at once on day one, the same bonuses produce far less compounded movement.
- **Profit share dwarfs revenue share.** In Hormozi's early-days numbers, Fast Cash Plays were ~15% of annual revenue and ~50% of profit, because warm-list margins approach 90%+ gross. The play looks marginal on a top-line view and dominant on a bottom-line view.

## Practical Use

Operator checklist when running a Fast Cash Play (synthesized from the playbook's tear-sheet on p. 18):

1. **Assemble the list.** Existing + previous customers in good standing + engaged leads. Exclude cooled-off leads (>6 months silent).
2. **Pick the offer.** Use the [[Hormozi-Unscalable-Value-Levers|unscalable value levers]] palette. 1–3 core components + 3 bonuses held in reserve. Build with the [[../01-100M-Offers/Hormozi-Offer-Creation|Offer Creation Process]].
3. **Set the price.** 10× to 50× current cart value. Bias toward bold; the price must shock.
4. **Set the cap.** 5–10% of customer base, or the number you can fulfill — whichever is lower. Must visibly sell out.
5. **Pick the sale pattern.** Consult or automated checkout. See [[Hormozi-Fast-Cash-Promo-Sequence|Fast Cash Promo Sequence]] for the matching campaign sequence.
6. **Pre-build all email + text copy and all bonuses *before* launch.** No improvisation mid-campaign — the bonuses are pre-assigned to specific days and must exist on those days.
7. **Run the sequence.** Send announcements with countdowns. On each touch, drip a new bonus and explain (a) the good it brings, (b) the bad it removes.
8. **Notify the list at sold-out** (and on any back-out spot opening). Sold-out announcement is the social-proof close that pays forward into the next quarter's play.
9. **Deliver the offer.** "You. Will. Work. More. For. These. Customers." The price is honored only if the value is honored.
10. **Collect the 4Rs post-delivery.** Reviews, Results, Referrals, Resells. (See [[Hormozi-Fast-Cash-Cadence|Fast Cash Cadence]].)
11. **Cool the list.** Return to value-providing rhythm before the next play.
12. **Set the next date.** Default cadence is 90 days — see [[Hormozi-Fast-Cash-Cadence|Cadence]].

For a low-tech operator: cards-on-file + "reply I'm in" + manual charge will run the entire mechanism without any additional infrastructure.

## Notes / Limits

- **Ultra-premium price requires ultra-premium value.** "I'm not giving anyone cell phone access!" misreads the lever. The point is not cell-phone access; the point is that the value must be crazy enough to justify the shock price. Substitute equally crazy components if the literal example does not fit the business.
- **Cooled-off leads are excluded for a reason.** Sending a Fast Cash Play to neglected leads erodes the operator's reputation more than it produces sales. Warm them up for the *next* play; keep this one to known-warm audiences.
- **Cap must be honest.** Faked spot counts collapse the lever the first time the market sees through them. Use an operationally true cap and enforce it. Wait-list the overflow at sold-out (capture payment commitment, charge if a spot opens).
- **Fast Cash is not a substitute for a working business.** The play multiplies the cash output of a list that already trusts the operator. It does not rescue a broken core offer or repair a churned-out audience.
- **Run cadence has limits.** Weekly is too frequent (lever erodes); annually is too rare (leaves money on the floor). Quarterly is the documented sweet spot — see [[Hormozi-Fast-Cash-Cadence|Fast Cash Cadence]].
- **Recurring "Max Cash" variant.** For subscription businesses, the offer can be "Monthly Price × 12 prepaid + bonuses" — a Fast Cash Play that monetizes prepay rather than a separate premium tier. Hormozi notes this variant typically converts ~10% with bonuses alone, no other offer changes needed.
