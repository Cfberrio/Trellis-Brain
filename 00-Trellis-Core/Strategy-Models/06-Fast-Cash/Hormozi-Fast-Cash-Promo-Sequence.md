# Hormozi — Fast Cash Promo Sequence

## Parent
- [[00-Book-Home|Fast Cash — Book Home]]

## Purpose
Operational sequence layer of the [[Hormozi-Fast-Cash-Play|Fast Cash Play]]. Defines the two campaign shapes Hormozi prescribes — Push-to-Consult (default for high-ticket) and Push-to-Automated-Checkout (for large lists or strong brands) — and the bonus-drip mechanic that makes both of them compound across the window. This is the "what gets sent on which day" layer; the offer body and pricing live in [[Hormozi-Fast-Cash-Play|Fast Cash Play]] and [[Hormozi-Unscalable-Value-Levers|Unscalable Value Levers]].

## Core Idea
> "Send each message with a countdown and a new bonus. Sell the bonus with explanations of the good stuff they get from it, and the bad stuff it gets rid of."

Two non-negotiables shape the sequence:
1. **Bonuses are pre-designed and held in reserve, not announced up front.** Each touch in the sequence reveals one new bonus. This is what gives a 7-day window 5–7 distinct reasons-to-buy instead of one stale pitch repeated.
2. **Texts pair with emails.** Texts draw attention to the email, which carries the detail. Neither channel runs alone; the pairing is the unit of communication.

The sequence is not "marketing copy choices." It is the structural mechanism that converts scarcity + urgency + bonuses into compounding pressure across a fixed window.

## Framework / Structure

### Choosing the variant

| | Push-to-Consult | Push-to-Automated-Checkout |
|---|---|---|
| Window | 7 days | ~5–6 days (shorter — stampede shape) |
| Cart open | **Day one**, sustained through the window | **Single moment** — built up to, then opened all at once |
| Sale flow | Prospect books a consult → consult closes the sale | Prospect goes to checkout page → buys in seconds |
| Best for | High-ticket, high-touch offers; small/medium lists; operators who can run consults | Large lists, automated infrastructure, established brand |
| Sales staff constraint | Spreads bookings across the window so a small team can meet everyone | Removes sales staff from the loop |
| Risk | Consult bottleneck if the offer hits viral on day one | Stampede infrastructure must hold — site / payments / capacity |

Hormozi's stated default: Push-to-Consult ("my bread and butter").

### PUSH TO CONSULT — 7-day sequence (pp. 9–10)

Day-by-day skeleton (text + email pairing):

| When | Action | Bonus reveal |
|---|---|---|
| **7 days out** | **Text only** — "something big is coming via email tomorrow." | — |
| **6 days out** | **Announcement email** — list 1–3 core components; price range optional ("four-figure investment" / "must be willing to invest at least $2,000" / "more than a year of service"); CTA = book consult. **Text** drawing attention to the email. | — |
| **5 days out** | **Recap email** — bullets of yesterday's announcement for missed readers. **Reveal Bonus 1** (e.g. Status — better parking). | Bonus 1 |
| **3 days out** | **Sales update** — units sold so far / units remaining; reinforce first-come-first-serve; restate good gained / bad avoided. **Text** with CTA to reply. | — |
| **2 days out** | **"X spots left" email** — **reveal Bonus 2** (e.g. Secrets — special training); explain the benefit. **Text** with CTA. | Bonus 2 |
| **24 hours out (AM)** | **"Warning: a few hours / X spots left"** — **reveal Bonus 3** (the strongest one — e.g. Access: in-person 2-day retreat at a luxury location). This bonus should be worth more than the entire price tag. **Text** with CTA. | Bonus 3 |
| **12 hours out (PM)** | **Sold out** — notify the list. **Text** to confirm. | — |
| **Optional — back-out** | If a buyer drops out, **"one spot left"** to the list. **Text** to confirm. | — |

Cart open day one. Booked consults distribute across the window so a single salesperson (often the operator) can take all of them.

### PUSH TO AUTOMATED CHECKOUT — stampede sequence (pp. 10–11)

| When | Action | Bonus reveal |
|---|---|---|
| **5 days out** | **Primer text** — "something big is coming tomorrow." | — |
| **4 days out** | **Announcement email** — 1–3 core components; price range optional. **Text** drawing attention. | — |
| **2 days out** | **"Cart opens in 48 hours"** email — restate first-come-first-serve. **Text** reminder. | — |
| **24 hours out** | **Reveal Bonus 1** (e.g. Personalization — extra customization); explain benefits; tell the list to set an alarm because the cart will sell out in minutes. **Text**. | Bonus 1 |
| **60 min out** | **Text** — set alarm, payment method ready. | — |
| **Cart open + 5 min** | **"Cart open" notification** — **reveal Bonus 2** (the strongest, instant-benefit bonus — e.g. Experience: operator's personal cell). The bonus is delivered the moment they buy. **Text**. | Bonus 2 |
| **+60 min** | **Sales update** — units sold, units left. **Text**. | — |
| **+6 hours** | **"X spots left"** — **reveal Bonus 3** (e.g. Convenience — extended hours); benefits; closes the offer. **Text**. | Bonus 3 |
| **+12 hours** | **Sold out**. **Text**. | — |
| **Next day — back-out** | If a buyer drops, **"one spot left"**. **Text**. | — |

Cart opens at one specific moment, not on day one. The build-up creates the stampede.

### The bonus-drip mechanic (the structural lever)
Both sequences pivot on the same idea: **3 pre-designed bonuses, dripped one per major touch, ascending in perceived value, with the strongest revealed in the final 24 hours.**

- Bonus 1 → introduced when momentum is needed (mid-window, after announcement).
- Bonus 2 → introduced when scarcity becomes visible ("X spots left").
- Bonus 3 → introduced at the final urgency moment, sized to be worth more than the full price (it should single-handedly close the remaining inventory).

All three bonuses are **honored for everyone who bought, regardless of which day they bought**. Early buyers get Bonuses 2 and 3 as a confirmation-of-decision, not a reward for waiting.

For each bonus, the message must explain: **(a) the good it brings, (b) the bad it removes**. Explanation, not just announcement, is what moves the perceived price-to-value gap.

### Sold-out and back-out notifications
These are not housekeeping — they are part of the sequence:
- **Sold-out announcement** — proves the promo was justified and feeds social proof into the next quarter's play. Hormozi treats this as required, not optional.
- **Back-out / "one spot left"** — reactivates the wait-list and provides a high-conversion final close.

### Sample copy (consult variant) — structural skeleton

The playbook ships ~6 emails + ~7 texts as fill-in-the-blank templates (pp. 19–26). Their structural pattern, reusable independent of the specific copy:

- **Email 1 (6 days out — Announcement)** — subject "We need to talk." Frames *why* the operator is running this offer ("I want to invest in another big thing that benefits you"); benefit list of the investment; defines the cap ("for [10] customers"); deadline ("EXPIRES SATURDAY MIDNIGHT PST"); savings + premium-features list; how-to-buy options (book call / call directly / reply with permission to charge card); PS reinforcing the cap and one-time nature.
- **Email 2 (5 days out — "Best deal ever")** — restates the offer; adds a sales update ("about half the spots are already gone"); reinforces buy mechanics.
- **Email 3 (3 days out — sales update)** — actual unit count remaining; reinforces "no other customers will get these" framing; gentle moral pressure ("easy to say you support something — another to show it").
- **Email 4 (2 days out — "2 spots left + game changer")** — reveal a previously-unmentioned bonus ("GAME CHANGER I WAS SAVING UNTIL NOW"); reinforces near-sold-out; "we'll probably hit the goal before the deadline" close.
- **Email 5 (Saturday AM — last day)** — "Don't kick yourself tomorrow"; addresses the "I'll wait until the next one" objection head-on; deadline restated; optional PS introducing one final last-day bonus (BONUS #5).
- **Email 6 (Saturday PM — sold-out / back-out)** — "RE: your goals we set" — reactivation message when a slot opens after sold-out; "REPLY YES" or "TEXT YES IM IN."

The text track pairs each email with a short, casual nudge and (on day 5) a meme-with-pattern-interrupt. Texts ask for thumbs-up engagement, link to scheduling, or directly request payment confirmation.

## Strategic Implications

- **The window is the offer.** A premium offer presented for 30 days with no urgency converts at a fraction of the same offer presented for 7 days. The clock is structural, not stylistic.
- **Bonus-drip turns a flat campaign into a compounding one.** A single Day-1 mega-email with all bonuses listed underperforms the same content split across 5–7 touches with one-bonus-per-touch. The reason: every touch must carry a *new* reason; restating yesterday's pitch produces fatigue, not movement.
- **Texts are not a redundancy; they are the attention layer.** Email carries detail; text carries attention. Operators who skip texts because "we already emailed" lose the read-rate that powers the email's effectiveness.
- **The sold-out announcement compounds across plays.** Each sold-out signal makes the next quarterly play more credible. Operators who run Fast Cash plays without announcing sold-out leave the social-proof asset on the floor.
- **Push-to-Consult is sales-staff-bottlenecked; Push-to-Auto is infrastructure-bottlenecked.** The choice is not stylistic — it is operational. Operators choose the variant whose bottleneck they can absorb.
- **Same offer, different sequence shape.** The offer body is identical between the two variants. Only the cart timing and call-to-action change. This means the operator does not need to redesign the offer when switching variants — only the sequence.

## Practical Use

Operator setup for either variant:

1. **Pick the variant.** Consult by default (most operators, most categories). Auto-checkout only if list size or brand strength makes it viable and infrastructure can hold a stampede.
2. **Write all emails and texts before the campaign starts.** No mid-campaign improvisation. Every touch is pre-built; each contains its assigned bonus reveal.
3. **Build all 3 bonuses in advance.** The day Bonus 3 is announced is too late to start building Bonus 3.
4. **Map bonuses to days deliberately.**
   - Strongest bonus → 24-hour window (Consult) or cart-open moment (Auto).
   - Mid-strength bonus → "X spots left" / sales-update touch.
   - Weakest bonus → introduction / momentum touch.
5. **Set sold-out trigger logic.** As soon as the cap is hit, fire the sold-out email and text — do not let inventory ambiguity persist.
6. **Wait-list overflow.** When buyers arrive late but had payment ready, capture commitment + permission to charge if a back-out opens. The back-out notification then becomes a high-conversion final close.
7. **Texts pair with emails 1:1.** Skip the temptation to "just email." Each major email gets a paired text; the paired text is the attention pull.
8. **Read the sample skeleton (pp. 19–26)** as a structural reference, not a copy template — fill the brackets with the operator's actual offer, but keep the structural beats (deadline, sales update, scarcity statement, sold-out announcement, back-out reactivation).
9. **For high-ticket recurring (e.g. annual prepay):** the consult variant carries the play; copy the announcement structure but lead with "a way to save more / get more on what you already pay for."

If the offer is correctly built, the playbook claim is **sell-out by day 3 or 4**. A campaign that has *not* sold out by day 4 is signaling that the offer is under-built — Hormozi's prescribed response is to add to the offer (extra bonus, more value) before sending the remaining touches, not to lower the price.

## Notes / Limits

- The bonus-drip only works if the bonuses are real. Promising a bonus that doesn't exist on the day it's revealed kills the play and damages the operator's reputation for future plays.
- Sequences are calendared as a default; the *real* clock is "until sold out." If the offer sells out on day 3, all subsequent touches collapse into "sold out" + "back-out / one spot left" notifications.
- The 7-day Consult window is the documented sweet spot. Longer → loses urgency; shorter → loses sales because consults can't all be scheduled. Don't compress for the sake of compressing.
- The Auto-checkout variant requires the audience to actually be able to find the cart in the open moment. If the list spans many time zones, schedule the cart-open at a time that fits the largest cohort, and accept that some buyers will be too late.
- "Sold out" + "back-out: one spot left" is a powerful pairing but is not infinitely reusable. Faking back-outs to keep the urgency running collapses trust faster than it generates incremental sales. Use only when an actual back-out occurs.
- The sample copy in the source uses casual / borderline-meme tone. The structural beats translate cleanly across tone registers; the casual tone does not. Operators in formal categories (B2B, regulated) keep the structure, swap the voice.
