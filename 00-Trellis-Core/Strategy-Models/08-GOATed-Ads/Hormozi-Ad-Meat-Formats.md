# Hormozi — Ad Meat Formats

## Parent
- [[00-Book-Home|GOATed Ads — Book Home]]

## Purpose
The five reusable ad-body formats Hormozi has used to win across services, education, physical products, brick-and-mortar, and software. Acts as the format palette for the meat layer of the [[Hormozi-Ad-Assembly-Process|Ad Assembly Process]] — the 3–5 meats produced per weekly recording session.

## Core Idea
The body of an ad ("the meat") fulfills the promise made by the hook. It aligns with the awareness level the hook targeted (see [[Hormozi-Ad-Awareness-Continuum|Awareness Continuum]]) and educates the prospect about the offer, the product, the solution, the problem, or the phenomenon — depending on where they entered the funnel.

Hormozi's claim: across years and industries, the ad bodies that won fell into exactly **five formats**. They are not exhaustive (others may work). They are battle-tested. **Mix and match is encouraged.**

> "The body of an ad gets rotated less often because fewer people see it. So you don't 'use it up' as often. Usually three to five per weekly recording session is more than enough."

## Framework / Structure

### The 5 formats

| # | Format | What it is | Sub-types |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Demonstration** | Show the product / service in use or in result | Product demo · service demo · unboxing · before-and-after / comparison · high-production hero ads (Dollar Shave Club / Old Spice class) |
| 2 | **Testimonial** | Customer or operator on camera providing proof | UGC · founder direct-to-camera · podcast-clip style · raw iPhone style · walk-and-talk rant · group testimonial / parade-of-proof · lifecycle ad · man-on-the-street · influencer collab |
| 3 | **Education** | Teach a concept that maps to the offer | Explainer videos · how-to / tutorial · whiteboard explainer · listicle · re-cut high-performing organic content |
| 4 | **Story** | Narrative-driven body | Storytelling · lifestyle · warnings & opportunities · documentary style · skit · brand manifesto · problem-solution arc |
| 5 | **Faceless** | No human presenter on camera | Customer-comment / text screenshots · text-only · slideshow · animation · cartoon · VFX-driven |

### Format → awareness fit (practical pairing)

The book does not assign a strict format-to-awareness mapping. The pairing follows from the hook style required at each awareness level (see [[Hormozi-Hook-By-Awareness|Hook By Awareness]]):

- **Most-Aware (offer-driven hooks):** Demonstration (show the deal), Faceless (text-overlay offer).
- **Product-Aware (proof-driven hooks):** Testimonial (especially parade-of-proof, group), Demonstration (before-and-after).
- **Solution-Aware (promise-driven hooks):** Education (explainer of the mechanism), Demonstration (the result delivered).
- **Problem-Aware (pain-driven hooks):** Story (problem-solution arc), Education (how-to).
- **Completely Unaware (curiosity-driven hooks):** Story (warnings & opportunities, documentary), Education (the explainer that reveals the unknown problem).

Use these pairings as defaults, not rules. Hormozi is explicit that mix-and-match is encouraged.

### Sub-type detail (preserved from source)

**Demonstration**
- Product demonstration — show the lead magnet / product literally while explaining it.
- Service demonstration — show the *result* of the service ("a packed gym").
- High-production hero ads — fast-paced, scene-switching, product + results throughout (Dollar Shave Club / Old Spice class).

**Testimonial**
- Podcast clip — pull a strong moment from a recorded conversation.
- Parade-of-proof — group testimonial with many people in frame; Hormozi's stated favorite group format.
- Lifecycle ad — see the *Marketing Machine Playbook* (external; not in this vault).
- Raw / iPhone style — single person, low production, direct-to-camera.
- Walk-and-talk rant — informal, location-based, opinionated.
- Man-on-the-street, influencer collab — third-party validation.

**Education**
- Whiteboard explainer — Hormozi's most-used education sub-type.
- How-to / listicle — sequential teaching of a process.
- Re-cut organic content — take a high-performing organic clip and run it as paid.

**Story**
- Storytelling / narrative — most flexible; broad application.
- Lifestyle — wealth / status / situation framing. Hormozi notes one lifestyle ad performed well "but I hated it" — the format can convert even when it conflicts with brand voice.
- Warnings and opportunities — pair naturally with Completely-Unaware curiosity hooks.
- Documentary, skit, brand manifesto — longer-form, stronger emotional load.

**Faceless**
- Customer-comment / text screenshots — run on their own as ads. Cheap to produce, high-trust signal.
- Text-only, slideshow, animation, cartoon, VFX — work when the operator does not want or cannot put a person on camera.

### Production cost ranking (lowest → highest)
- Faceless < Education (whiteboard) < raw Testimonial < Story (skit / narrative) < high-production Demonstration / hero.

This ordering matters for the weekly cadence: under time constraint, prioritize Faceless and raw Testimonial first to preserve the 3–5 meats per session output target.

## Strategic Implications

- **The meat is the slow-rotating part.** The same meat survives more impressions than the same hook because fewer viewers reach it. Three to five per session is enough; the assembly line's variance comes from hook layer, not meat layer.
- **Format coverage > format optimization.** Picking the "best" single format is the wrong frame. Running 3–5 meats *across different formats* per session insures against any single format burning out and gives the operator multiple winners across audience segments.
- **Faceless is undervalued.** Customer-text-screenshot ads run as standalone ads convert without any presenter, any production, any travel. They are the highest-leverage format the median operator under-uses. Hormozi calls them out twice in the source.
- **High-production hero is rarely necessary, often overdone.** Old Spice and Dollar Shave Club are the cited examples — both had budgets the median operator does not have. Lower-production raw / podcast / parade-of-proof formats win consistently with a fraction of the cost.
- **The brand-voice override fails.** Hormozi explicitly notes a lifestyle ad he "hated" still performed. The format selection should be driven by performance data, not by founder aesthetics.
- **Mix and match is the source's explicit guidance.** A walk-and-talk rant *with* embedded customer screenshots *with* a whiteboard moment is allowed and often outperforms any pure single-format ad.

## Practical Use

When picking the 3–5 meats for a weekly session:

1. **Start from the awareness distribution of this week's 50 hooks.** If hooks lean cold-end (Problem-Aware / Completely-Unaware), bias meats toward Story and Education. If hooks lean warm-end (Most-Aware / Product-Aware), bias toward Demonstration and Testimonial.
2. **Always include at least one Faceless meat.** Lowest production cost, highest survival rate, runnable without scheduling on-camera time.
3. **Always include at least one Testimonial meat.** Either record a customer, or pull a podcast clip, or assemble a parade-of-proof from existing footage.
4. **Use the sub-types as a checklist.** When stuck, scan the sub-type list above and pick the unused one with lowest production cost.
5. **Mix formats inside a single meat where it serves the message.** Demonstration + screenshots is a legitimate single meat.
6. **Do not chase production polish per meat.** Production polish does not survive the combinatorial filter — Hormozi's wins were predominantly raw / iPhone / podcast / walk-and-talk style. Save high-production hero for confirmed winners worth amplifying.
7. **Track which format won.** Each weekly batch is a small experiment in format performance. Over weeks, the operator builds a format-to-awareness map specific to their offer.

## Notes / Limits

- **The 5-format list is not exhaustive.** Hormozi: "I'm not saying these are the only formats. I'm just saying these are the ones I've used that worked." Do not treat the list as a closed taxonomy.
- **Lifecycle ads require an external mechanism.** The lifecycle sub-type points to the *Marketing Machine Playbook*. That playbook is not extracted in this vault. Operators implementing lifecycle ads should source the mechanism externally before using the sub-type.
- **High-production demos require capital.** The hero-ad sub-type is real but capital-intensive. The median operator should reach scale on lower-production formats first.
- **Format alone does not save a weak hook or a weak offer.** A great Demonstration ad on a bad offer still loses money. The meat layer compounds creative value; it does not create it from nothing.
- **Some sub-types depend on prior assets.** Parade-of-proof, group testimonial, lifecycle ads all require an existing customer base willing to be on camera or a content history to mine. New operators bias toward Faceless and raw Testimonial first.
