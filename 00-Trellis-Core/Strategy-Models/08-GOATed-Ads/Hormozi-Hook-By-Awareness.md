# Hormozi — Hook By Awareness

## Parent
- [[00-Book-Home|GOATed Ads — Book Home]]

## Purpose
The 50-hook build at the front of the [[Hormozi-Ad-Assembly-Process|Ad Assembly Process]]. Defines (a) the five places hooks come from, in priority order, and (b) the five hook *styles* mapped to the five awareness levels of the [[Hormozi-Ad-Awareness-Continuum|Awareness Continuum]]. Operationalizes the 80% of prep time the playbook says to spend on hooks.

Scope is **paid ads**. For word-level hook construction, the all-time hook library, and the cross-surface 70/20/10 distribution rule, see the canonical *Hooks* book: [[../07-Hooks/Hormozi-Hook-Library|Hook Library]], [[../07-Hooks/Hormozi-Hook-Types-Palette|Hook Types Palette]], [[../07-Hooks/Hormozi-Hook-Definition|Hook Definition]], [[../07-Hooks/Hormozi-Hook-70-20-10-Process|Hook 70/20/10 Process]]. The author explicitly distinguishes the two scopes (GOATed Ads p. 6).

## Core Idea
Hooks are not invented — they are **sourced and adapted**. The 50-hook target per session is a portfolio: previous winners (for immediate results) plus expansion hooks (to reach colder, larger slices). Each hook is shaped to the awareness level of the slice it targets.

> "If 90% of your hooks land in the 'Most aware' bucket, spread them out to capture a bigger slice. When in doubt, go a little broader."

## Framework / Structure

### Layer 1 — Where to source hooks (5 places, priority order)

For *immediate results* (warm-side wins from existing creative + content):

1. **Winning hooks from your previous ads.** Past winners are reused. Over time you develop a stable. One good hook can carry surprising amounts of runway. *Skip if first time running ads.*
2. **Winning hooks from your free content.** Take hooks from longs, shorts, tweets, emails that performed organically and use them as paid-ad hooks. Highest likelihood of converting because the hook is already audience-validated.
3. **Winning hooks from other people's ads.** Save ads you like; write down their hooks. If broad enough to apply to your offer, use them. *"Winning hooks in one industry often work in others."*
4. **Winning hooks from other people's free content.** Find content with massive view counts in your space. Snag the hook.
5. **Platform ad libraries.** Last resort. Pros: bajillion ads visible. Cons: most are bad and you cannot tell winners from losers. Use only for inspiration from companies you already know run great paid ads.

**Operational note.** Hormozi keeps a phone album for saved competitor ads.

### Layer 2 — Expansion hooks: hook style by awareness level

When existing winners cap (CAC rises with spend), expand hook coverage across awareness levels. The hook *style* changes with the level:

| Awareness level | Audience state | Hook style | Hook example (template) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Most Aware | Knows product, only needs deal | **Offer-driven** | "50% off our best-selling XYZ product for the rest of the month." |
| 2. Product-Aware | Knows what you sell, unsure it fits | **Proof-driven** | "Discover why 10,000 people chose XYZ to solve their [problem] last month." |
| 3. Solution-Aware | Knows result wanted, doesn't know your product delivers it | **Promise-driven** | "The fastest way to [achieve desired result] — introducing XYZ." |
| 4. Problem-Aware | Senses problem, doesn't know solutions exist | **Pain-driven** | "Tired of [specific problem]? There's a better way." |
| 5. Completely Unaware | Doesn't know problem exists | **Curiosity-driven** | "The hidden danger in your daily routine that's costing you [money/time/health]." |

### Worked translations (from the source)

**B2C — XFast weight-loss shake:**
1. Most Aware: "XFast's new formula: now with 25% more protein — same great taste!"
2. Product-Aware: "Why 9 out of 10 XFast users reached their goal weight within 3 months."
3. Solution-Aware: "Lose 15 pounds in 30 days with our scientifically proven meal replacement system."
4. Problem-Aware: "Frustrated with crash diets that don't last? There's a sustainable way to shed pounds."
5. Completely Unaware: "The hidden hormonal imbalance that's making 1 in 3 Americans gain weight."

**B2B — DigitalBoost marketing agency:**
1. Most Aware: "DigitalBoost: now offering social media management at 20% off for new clients."
2. Product-Aware: "See how DigitalBoost increased ROI by 150% for 5 different industries."
3. Solution-Aware: "Double your online sales in 6 months with our data-driven marketing strategies."
4. Problem-Aware: "Is your website getting sales? You might be missing these crucial elements."
5. Completely Unaware: "The unexpected way your business is losing $1000s each month in untapped revenue."

### Layer 3 — Pro Tip: meme-like / widest hooks
Memes (or audience-specific subculture memes for narrower targets) attract the largest slice possible. They work like "a moth to a flame" — explode the eyeball count exposed to the ad. Test them. Hormozi tests them himself.

### Layer 4 — Distribution rule for the 50
- Spread the 50 across awareness buckets, biased toward the levels you have *not* yet saturated.
- **Default bias:** if 90% of your hooks live in Most-Aware, redistribute. Go broader. You still catch the warm slice and earn cold attention too.
- **Promise-driven and curiosity-driven hooks add specificity.** Specific outcomes ("15 pounds in 30 days") and specific dangers ("1 in 3 Americans") outperform vague versions.

## Strategic Implications

- **Hooks are sourced, then varied.** The 50/week target is reachable because the inputs are existing winners + audience-validated organic content + adjacent-industry winners. Treating each hook as a from-scratch creative writing task makes the cadence impossible.
- **Cross-platform hook arbitrage is one of the highest-leverage moves.** A hook that earned attention organically (where impressions are free and feedback is honest) is the strongest candidate for paid-ad scale. Most operators skip this.
- **Awareness mapping is what turns "write 50 hooks" into a structured task.** Without the mapping, hook lists drift to whatever the operator finds clever. With it, the operator forces coverage across the continuum.
- **Specificity beats cleverness at every level.** Numbers, timeframes, named segments, named dangers — these out-convert generic phrasing in every bucket. The hook templates above are scaffolds; the operator's job is to fill them with specific, true claims.
- **Memes are not a gimmick at scale.** They are the cheapest legitimate way to reach the widest audience. Operators who refuse to test them on aesthetic grounds leave the broadest slice on the table.
- **Hook saturation is the *first* metric to suspect when CAC rises.** The hook is the first three seconds; it burns out fastest. If a winning ad's CAC starts climbing, replacing the hook (same meat, same CTA) is the fastest test.

## Practical Use

For each weekly session, build the hook prep document in this order:

**Sources pass (~30–40 min):**
1. Pull your last 8–12 weeks of paid-ad performance. Extract the top-performing hooks. Drop into the doc.
2. Open your last 8–12 weeks of organic content. Extract any high-view opening lines / titles / first frames. Drop into the doc.
3. Open your saved-ads album / swipe file. Extract any hook with broad applicability.
4. Open 2–3 high-view organic accounts in your space (or adjacent). Extract opening lines.
5. (Optional) Quick scan of platform ad libraries for 2–3 known good advertisers.

**Awareness pass (~30–40 min):**
6. Tag each candidate hook by awareness level (1–5).
7. Identify gaps. If most candidates are Most-Aware or Product-Aware, write expansion hooks for Solution-Aware, Problem-Aware, Completely-Unaware until coverage is balanced.
8. Add 2–5 meme-style or widest-possible hooks deliberately.
9. Trim and rewrite each hook for specificity (concrete numbers, timeframes, named segments).
10. Stop at 50.

**Output:** a numbered list of 50 hooks, each tagged by awareness level and source bucket. Hand off to the recording session.

For first-time advertisers: skip Source #1, lean on Sources #2–#5, and bias the 50 toward Solution-Aware and Problem-Aware (mid-curve hooks usually establish profitable scale fastest for unbranded operators).

## Notes / Limits

- **Past-winner reuse plateaus.** Reusing the same handful of winning hooks works until the audience burns out on them. The expansion-hook discipline exists precisely to refresh the pipeline before that happens.
- **Schwartz buckets are messy in reality.** Hormozi is explicit. The 5-level table is a thinking tool. Real prospects sit on a continuum and can move between levels mid-funnel. Use the awareness mapping to *vary* hooks, not to gate prospects.
- **Branded operators have one extra option.** A brand with established awareness (Subway-class) can run offer-driven hooks to cold national audiences profitably. Unbranded operators cannot — the hook has to do the awareness work. See [[Hormozi-Ad-Awareness-Continuum|Awareness Continuum]] for the brand-spend edge case.
- **Memes have failure modes.** A meme that lands cleanly on a subculture but reads as inauthentic from the brand will tank engagement and trust. Test them, but ground them in the audience's actual culture, not generic internet humor.
- **Hooks are not headlines or subject lines wholesale.** A hook for paid video ads usually pairs with a visual; a strong text-only hook may not survive transposition. Test before assuming portability.
