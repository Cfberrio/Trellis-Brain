# Hormozi — Communications Scrape

## Parent
- [[00-Book-Home|Marketing Machine — Book Home]]

## Purpose
Node 4 of the Marketing Machine. Defines a system for harvesting written customer signal — support/account-rep chats, community posts and comments, and external review sites — and converting it into screenshot- and screen-recording-format ad creative. The node operates on text the business is already generating; the engineering work is keyword-searchability, folder discipline, and a manager-level reward loop that makes reps actually surface the wins.

## Core Idea
> *"This process weaponizes all those great little moments during those interactions, then bundles them into a dynamite stick of proof."*

The Communications Scrape is a three-source pipeline with one shared mechanism. The three sources are: (1) Support / Account Rep chat threads, (2) the operator's own customer community, (3) external review sites. The shared mechanism is: **keyword search → screenshot → labeled folder → manager reward loop.** Skipping any of the four steps collapses the node — Hormozi is explicit that without manager reinforcement, reps will not do it.

## Framework / Structure

### The three sources

**Source 1 — Support / Account Rep Chats.** Direct messages between customers and customer-facing reps. The yield is screenshots of customer-typed wins ("just closed", "$X in profit", "first PIF", etc.). Deep, authentic, and dated.

**Source 2 — Community Wins / Posts.** Comments and posts inside the brand-owned community. The yield is both individual screenshots and **screen-recordings of scrolling through a "wins" tab or a comment thread** — a high-leverage format because it shows volume of social proof in a single asset.

**Source 3 — Reviews & Comments on external sites.** 5-star reviews and high-engagement comments on third-party platforms. Hormozi's enumerated list: Glassdoor, Yelp, Google, Facebook, Trustpilot, Amazon, etc. Same screenshot-or-screen-recording output.

### The keyword-search mechanism
Every source uses the same query mechanism: a defined list of "win" keywords. Hormozi's Gym Launch list (verbatim): **PIF, Sales, $, Profit, Revenue, Monthly, close, closed, leads, show, scheduled, win.** The list is brand-specific — for a fitness business it would be weight, lbs, before/after, scale, etc. For a B2B SaaS it would be deals, pipeline, MQLs, conversion, retention, etc.

### The folder structure
A single shared folder, accessible to all support reps. Optional sub-folders by content type: **sales, marketing, profit, specific programs, tiers of service.** Inside the community variant, a post category called "wins" if the platform supports it.

### The reward loop
Hormozi is explicit and blunt: *"If you don't reward them for doing it, they won't do it."*

Two layers:
1. **Manager-side recognition.** A manager checks who turned in screenshots each cycle and gives kudos. This is the floor.
2. **Optional bonus.** A $100 gift card to anyone whose submitted screenshot becomes a winning ad — typically tracked weekly. This converts win-spotting from chore to micro-economy.

### The community-specific cadence
Communities have a weekly rhythm Hormozi prescribes:
- **Friday:** post the prompt encouraging customers to comment with their "wins of the week."
- **Friday:** add the win-of-the-week post to the community manager's end-of-week checklist.
- **Monday:** start-of-week scrape — screenshot the wins that came in over the weekend.

### The bonus screen-recording format
For the community source, Hormozi calls out a specific high-yield asset: **screen-record yourself scrolling through the comments / wins / weekly thread**. It produces an additional ad on top of the individual screenshots, because the volume is itself the proof.

## Strategic Implications

- **Volume of proof is itself a creative format.** A single screenshot is one data point; a 30-second scroll-recording of fifty screenshots is a different ad entirely. Operators who only capture individual shots leave the higher-yield format untapped.
- **The keyword list is a one-time asset.** Once defined, it works forever for the business. Refresh annually as offers and language evolve, but the time investment is small relative to the recurring weekly yield.
- **Reps are the bottleneck, not the data.** The data exists in chat platforms regardless. The constraint is whether the rep takes 30 seconds to screenshot when they see a win. Without the manager reward loop, the rep optimizes for closing the next ticket, not for capturing creative — and the node silently fails.
- **External reviews are the most defensible source.** Support chats and community posts can be dismissed as cherry-picked. Public 5-star reviews on Yelp / Google / Trustpilot have third-party credibility. They are also the source most operators forget exists — a calendar reminder per site, sorted by newest, is the entire workflow.
- **The community's "wins" tab is the single highest-leverage configuration.** If the platform supports a category, create one called "wins." Customers self-sort their proof into the bucket. The screen-recording is then a single capture of the tab — the entire ad is "look at this volume."
- **The "gift card per winning ad" mechanic is asymmetric.** $100 to a rep whose screenshot becomes a working ad costs the business a tiny fraction of the LTV that ad generates. The same logic Hormozi applies to handler bonuses at events ([[Hormozi-Event-Capture-Playbook|Event Capture Playbook]]) applies here.

## Practical Use

Communications Scrape install checklist (consolidated from pp. 16–18 + tear-out):

**Source 1 — Support / Account Rep Chats**
1. Define the keyword list. Pick the words customers use when good things happen in your business.
2. Create the shared support folder with read/write access for all reps.
3. Add a recurring task to the team checklist: search all keywords in the chat platform.
4. Sort by date. Screenshot the new ones since the last scrape.
5. Drop screenshots into the shared folder. Optional sub-folders by category.
6. Manager: weekly check on who submitted, public kudos for participation.
7. Optional: $100 gift card to any rep whose submitted screenshot becomes a winning ad in the week.

**Source 2 — Community Wins / Posts**
1. If the community platform supports post categories, create one called "wins." If not, skip the category and rely on keyword search.
2. Friday: prompt customers to post their "wins of the week."
3. Monday: screenshot weekend wins.
4. Run the same keyword search inside the community, sort by newest, screenshot the best.
5. **Bonus asset:** screen-record yourself scrolling through the wins tab or the weekly thread. Save as additional ad creative.

**Source 3 — Reviews & Comments Scrape**
1. List every site the business has reviews on. Hormozi's starter list: Glassdoor, Yelp, Google, Facebook, Trustpilot, Amazon.
2. Create a single review-site folder.
3. Calendar reminder per site: visit, sort by newest, screenshot the good ones.
4. Drop into the folder. Manager checks the folder, rewards the contributor.

## Notes / Limits

- **No reward = no submissions.** Hormozi is unambiguous about this: reps will not surface wins on their own time. The reward loop is the workflow. Skip it and the node produces nothing.
- **Folders are not ads.** Hormozi's mid-section reminder (p. 19): *"adding stuff to folders isn't gonna get you more customers. You need to use all that stuff in your advertisements!"* The scrape only matters if marketing is pulling from the folder weekly. See [[Hormozi-Marketing-Machine-Cadence|Marketing Machine Cadence]] for the consumption rhythm.
- **Platform changes break searches.** Some chat platforms restrict keyword search at the customer-message level. Verify the search works *before* training reps — and have a fallback (e.g., asking reps to flag with an emoji that becomes the search target).
- **Community-platform constraints.** Not every community platform supports custom post categories. If yours doesn't, use a hashtag convention or a pinned weekly thread instead. Do not let the missing feature kill the node.
- **External review timing.** Reviews bunch up after launches, refunds, or PR moments. Set a reminder closer to weekly during launch periods, monthly during steady state.
- **Privacy on screenshots.** Crop names, profile photos, and any identifying details if the customer has not given explicit ad-permission. Default-anonymized screenshots are still strong proof and avoid the consent problem.
