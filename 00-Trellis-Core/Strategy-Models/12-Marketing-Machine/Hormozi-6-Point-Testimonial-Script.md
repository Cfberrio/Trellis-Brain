# Hormozi — 6-Point Testimonial Script

## Parent
- [[00-Book-Home|Marketing Machine — Book Home]]

## Purpose
Reusable extraction script that drives every depth-of-story testimonial in the Marketing Machine. Takes a customer through a 6-step arc that captures both the internal and external sides of struggle and victory, plus the explicit moment of conversion. Used inside [[Hormozi-Event-Capture-Playbook|Event Capture Playbook]] (professional testimonials + rapid-fire MITS), [[Hormozi-Bonus-And-Award-Mechanism|Bonus & Award Mechanism]] (unlock-trade videos), and [[Hormozi-Testimonial-Competition|Testimonial Competition]] (submission template). Lifted into its own canonical note because the same six points are referenced by four different production contexts in the book.

## Core Idea
> *"Walk them through the same 6-point script."*

A testimonial that only describes external metrics — "I made $X" — produces weak ad creative. A testimonial that only describes internal feeling — "I'm so much happier now" — also produces weak ad creative. The 6-point script forces both sides at both ends of the journey, plus the *moment of decision*, which is the conversion-driving piece most testimonials skip.

## Framework / Structure

### The six points (verbatim from p. 15)

| # | Point | Question prompts |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Internal Struggle** | What was rock bottom before doing this? What did you have to do that you hated? What things did you want to do that you couldn't? |
| 2 | **External Struggle** | What were your objective metrics? |
| 3 | **Skepticism** | What were your main concerns about buying / clicking / joining? |
| 4 | **Overcome** | What made you do it anyways? |
| 5 | **External Victory** | What are your objective metrics now? |
| 6 | **Internal Victory** | What's been the best moment since? What can you do now you couldn't do before? What things did you hate doing you don't have to do anymore? |

### The arc, structurally
- **Points 1 & 6 are the bookends** — internal-feeling rock-bottom paired with internal-feeling victory. These are the emotional payload.
- **Points 2 & 5 are the spine** — objective metrics before / objective metrics after. These are the credibility payload.
- **Points 3 & 4 are the hinge** — the skepticism → overcome moment. This is the conversion payload, and it is the part most "before-and-after" testimonials drop.

The script works because it does not let the subject choose to skip the hinge. Skepticism + Overcome are explicitly asked because that is the section a viewing prospect identifies with the most: *"I had the same hesitation. Here is what got them past it."*

### Hook protocol
The 6 points are the body of the testimonial. The **hook** is its own unit, captured separately and separately tested. Hormozi's instruction (p. 16):

> *"The most important part of the testimonial is the hook. Be explicit. You can have them say a few different hooks to see what works better before they get into their story."*

Have the subject try multiple hook variants on tape before going into Point 1. In post, the editor selects the strongest. See [[../07-Hooks/Hormozi-Hook-Definition|Hook Definition]] for the canonical hook framing and [[../07-Hooks/Hormozi-Hook-Types-Palette|Hook Types Palette]] for the variant menu.

### Two execution modes

**Long-form mode (Professional Testimonials).** All six points, full answers, conversational pace. Total runtime typically 10–15 minutes raw. Used at events and for award unlocks.

**Rapid-fire mode (Man-in-the-Street).** All six points asked of one person, fast pace, total runtime under 2 minutes. Edited to weave the same characters across a montage. Hormozi's framing:

> *"If you do the 6-point script. Ask all six questions to each person but in a rapid fire manner. These interviews can take less than 2 minutes. But you can hit all six questions and weave the same characters through the testimonial reel."*

The rapid-fire mode produces what Hormozi calls a "mondo testimonial" — a single edit that captures multiple subjects answering each of the six points in turn, generating consensus around every beat of the arc.

### Production-side rules
- **Subject says their name at the start of the video.** Camera teams often don't know the subject; saying the name on tape is the matching mechanism in post.
- **Hooks first, body second** — same tape, same session.
- **Print the script for the camera team** so they can prompt if the subject loses the thread.
- **Use the script identically across all contexts** — events, awards, competitions, bonus unlocks. The script is the standard; the context is the variable.

## Strategic Implications

- **Internal-external pairing buys both empathy and credibility.** A testimonial with only metrics reads as a sales pitch. A testimonial with only emotion reads as marketing. The script forces both, in the same arc, in the same subject's voice — the format prospects actually trust.
- **The skepticism-overcome hinge is the conversion piece.** This is the single highest-impact decision in the script. Most operators allow testimonial subjects to drift past it ("…and so I joined and now…"). The explicit question forces the subject to say what their hesitation was and what got them past it. That sentence is what closes the prospect watching.
- **Hook-first capture is non-negotiable.** A testimonial with the right body and the wrong opening 3–5 seconds gets scrolled past. Capturing 3–5 hook variants per subject costs 60 extra seconds and triples the editor's options.
- **One script, multiple production contexts.** The same six points serve a 12-minute event testimonial, a 90-second MITS rapid-fire, a 5-minute bonus-unlock email submission, and a competition entry. The asset reuse is structural, not coincidental — Hormozi designed the script for context-portability.
- **Rapid-fire weave is more persuasive than single-subject long-form, on volume.** A long-form testimonial is one trusted source. A weave of five subjects answering each of the six points is consensus across five sources. Same total runtime, more credibility units.
- **The script is reusable across brands.** It is structurally a refactor of the standard "before-and-after" arc with the skepticism hinge added. Any business with a customer outcome can use it. Brand-specific changes are wording, not structure.

## Practical Use

Pre-shoot:
1. Print the 6-point script with question prompts. One copy per camera team, one for the handler, one taped near the subject's chair.
2. Print 5–10 candidate hooks for the subject to try (lifted from [[../07-Hooks/Hormozi-Hook-Types-Palette|Hook Types Palette]]).

Capture (per subject):
3. Subject says their name on tape.
4. Subject reads or improvises 3–5 hook variants. One take each.
5. Walk the subject through the 6 points in order. Allow conversational answers; use the prompts when the subject narrows the answer too quickly.
6. If running rapid-fire mode, set a 2-minute timer and move briskly.

Edit:
7. Pick the strongest hook. Front-load it.
8. Cut the body to the 6-point arc, in order, trimming hesitations.
9. For event reels, weave multiple subjects' answers point-by-point: all subjects answering Point 1, then all answering Point 2, etc.
10. Match-merge with names captured on tape.

For Bonus Unlock submissions (pp. 19–20): the customer self-records to the same 6-point script and emails the file. The script is the submission template.

For Competition entries (p. 22): the script is attached to the competition rules. Submissions that follow the structure are eligible.

## Notes / Limits

- **Do not reorder the points.** The arc is internal-struggle → external-struggle → skepticism → overcome → external-victory → internal-victory. Reordering breaks the emotional pacing — internal opening builds connection, external metrics earn credibility, the hinge converts, and internal close anchors memory.
- **Skepticism question can feel awkward.** Some subjects will deflect ("oh, I had no concerns"). Re-ask: "what almost made you not buy?" — the question is too valuable to skip on a refusal.
- **Subject coaching trade-off.** Coaching the *structure* (the 6 points, the hook test) is correct. Coaching the *content* (telling the subject what to say at each point) destroys the asset. Hold the line.
- **Rapid-fire is not skim-fire.** Sub-2-minute capture still requires the subject to answer all six points specifically. A subject who can't articulate the points fast belongs in long-form mode for that interview.
- **Hook variants need to feel natural.** Subject struggling to deliver a written hook will read flatter than a subject riffing in their own words. Use the variants as starting points, not scripts.
- **The script does not replace consent.** Confirm in writing that the recording will be used in advertising. Same compliance posture as [[Hormozi-Lifecycle-Ads|Lifecycle Ads]] and the rest of the Marketing Machine.
