# Hormozi — Bonus & Award Mechanism

## Parent
- [[00-Book-Home|Marketing Machine — Book Home]]

## Purpose
Node 5 of the Marketing Machine. Defines two incentive-driven UGC mechanisms that trade something the customer wants (a paid bonus or a status award) for testimonial proof. The first — Bonus Unlocks — uses a saleable item as the trade currency. The second — Award Testimonials — generates *four separate forms of proof from a single award per customer*, making it the single highest-yield asset in the Marketing Machine per unit of customer effort.

## Core Idea
> *"Instead of just giving awards out, I should ask people to submit testimonials to get the award."*

The mechanism's logic: customers who would normally accept an award or pay for a bonus already have the testimonial in them. They just don't volunteer it. By gating the bonus or award on a structured submission, the operator turns a one-way giveaway into a two-way exchange that produces ad creative as a byproduct. The *receipt* of the award becomes additional proof on top of the receipt's content.

## Framework / Structure

The node has two distinct sub-mechanisms.

### Sub-mechanism 1 — Bonus Unlocks

**The setup.** Create a giveaway worth real money — a training, a one-time access, a tiny project, a status tier. Hormozi's examples for Gym Launch: sales scripts, ad-platform training. Build the asset, attach a price, list it for sale.

**The trade.** Pitch it normally. Then offer the alternative path: instead of paying, the customer can unlock it free by submitting a testimonial video. Put the recorded pitch where all customers see it.

**The submission requirements.** Hormozi specifies the unlock checklist:
- Send a video under 5 minutes hitting the [[Hormozi-6-Point-Testimonial-Script|6-point script]] to a dedicated email (e.g., `proof@yourco.com`).
- Reference video provided (the operator's best testimonial to date) as the example to model on.
- Post the video in the community with stats attached. **This generates a second testimonial in written format from the same submission.**
- Tag the community manager in the post.
- Include other keywords to make the post easy to scrape later (ties back into [[Hormozi-Communications-Scrape|Communications Scrape]]).

**Why the trade works.** The bonus has a real price tag. Customers either pay or trade. Either path produces business value. The trade path produces ad creative; the pay path produces revenue. The operator wins both ways.

### Sub-mechanism 2 — Award Testimonials (4-Proofs system)

**The setup.** Create an aspirational milestone for customers (Gym Launch's was hitting $1M/year as a gym owner). The milestone must be:
- Genuinely meaningful to the customer base.
- Tied to a status object — a trophy, model, or community-specific symbol. *"Make it big and cool. Spend some money on it."*
- Branded to the community itself (Gym Launch's award was a sword-and-shield, because their community was called "Gym Lords" and swords are common in fitness).

**The redemption requirement.** To receive the award, the customer must produce four separate forms of proof.

| # | Proof | Format | Where it lives |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Sales / metric proof post in the community | Written + screenshots, using the operator's testimonial template, tagging the community owner | Community feed (scrapable, cf. [[Hormozi-Communications-Scrape\|Communications Scrape]]) |
| 2 | 6-point testimonial video | Video email to `proof@yourco.com`, following the [[Hormozi-6-Point-Testimonial-Script\|6-point script]] | Inbox / asset library |
| 3 | Unboxing video | Customer-recorded reaction to receiving the award | Inbox / asset library |
| 4 | On-stage / in-person presentation video | Operator-recorded handover at the next event | Event capture, see [[Hormozi-Event-Capture-Playbook\|Event Capture Playbook]] |

**Award production split.** Two versions of the award:
- **Personalized / engraved** — mailed to the customer, drives the unboxing video.
- **Generic** — held back for the on-stage presentation moment.

This split is structural, not cosmetic — it lets Proofs 3 and 4 happen on different timelines.

### Announcement video
A separate produced video announces the award to the community: what it is, what milestone unlocks it, what the customer receives, and what they need to submit to redeem it. Customers can't aspire to an award they don't know exists.

## Strategic Implications

- **The Award is the highest-yield single mechanism in the Marketing Machine.** One milestone-hitter produces four proof artifacts: written + scrapable, video testimonial, unboxing reaction, on-stage presentation. No other node produces four formats from one customer interaction.
- **Bonus Unlocks weaponize the price tag.** The bonus has to be saleable on its own merits — that's what makes the trade meaningful. A customer wouldn't trade a 5-minute testimonial for a $20 bonus, but they will trade for a $500 training. Pricing the bonus is what creates the incentive curve.
- **The community-post requirement is the silent multiplier.** Both mechanisms require the customer to *also* post in the community. That single requirement creates ongoing organic-feed proof, future scrape inventory, and pressure-of-precedent on other customers. Operators who skip the community-post requirement leave the second-order yield uncaptured.
- **Award status > award value.** The trophy / sword / model is symbolic. Customers do not redeem for cash value — they redeem for recognition in front of their peer community. Hormozi's framing: *"People have to crave this thing."* A boring award produces no submissions; a community-symbolic award produces a queue.
- **The unboxing reaction is a structural innovation.** Most reaction videos in marketing are organic-and-rare. The Award system *manufactures* the unboxing moment by mailing a personalized object after the customer commits to film it. The reaction is built into the redemption flow.
- **The on-stage handover compounds with [[Hormozi-Event-Capture-Playbook|Event Capture Playbook]].** Award presentations at events become proof for future events ("here's last year's winner getting the sword"), which makes the next milestone more aspirational, which produces more submissions. The cycle is self-reinforcing once the first cohort is awarded.
- **Asymmetric economics.** A trophy + shipping costs a few hundred dollars. The four-asset proof stack feeds ad accounts for months. The mechanism's CAC-reduction value vastly exceeds its production cost.

## Practical Use

### Bonus Unlocks install
1. **Build the bonus.** Pick something real (training, recording, tiny project, tier access). Make it saleable on its own merits.
2. **Price it.** Real price tag, not a token amount.
3. **Record the pitch.** Sell it normally. Post the recording where all customers see it.
4. **Define the trade rule.** Document the unlock conditions:
   - Video under 5 minutes following the [[Hormozi-6-Point-Testimonial-Script|6-point script]].
   - Sent to `proof@yourco.com` (or equivalent dedicated address).
   - Reference video provided as a model.
   - Customer also posts in the community with stats, tagging the community manager.
   - Customer includes scrape-friendly keywords in the post.
5. **Acknowledge submissions.** Manual reply confirms the unlock. Deliver the bonus.
6. **Route assets.** Video → ad library. Community post → scrape pipeline.

### Award Testimonials install
1. **Pick the milestone.** Aspirational, measurable, real for the customer base.
2. **Design the trophy.** Big, cool, community-symbolic, branded. Spend money on it.
3. **Produce the announcement video.** What the award is, what unlocks it, what the customer receives, what they need to submit.
4. **Order two versions of the award per recipient.** Personalized (mailed) + generic (held for stage).
5. **Document the 4-proof submission requirements.**
   - Proof 1: community post with metric template, tagging the community owner.
   - Proof 2: 6-point video to `proof@yourco.com`.
   - Proof 3: unboxing video upon receiving the personalized award.
   - Proof 4: on-stage / in-person handover, captured by event camera team.
6. **Mail the personalized award.** Trigger Proof 3.
7. **Hold the generic award.** Use at the next event to capture Proof 4. See [[Hormozi-Event-Capture-Playbook|Event Capture Playbook]].
8. **Route all four proofs.** Each goes to the correct asset library and into the ad-assembly queue.

### Tear-out checklist (pp. 27–30) cross-reference
- **Bonus Unlock Ads:** videos in email + screenshots.
- **Award Unlock Ads:** videos in email + unboxing videos + screenshots + stage videos and pictures (Proof 4 lives in Event Ads as well — both nodes are correct).

## Notes / Limits

- **The bonus must be saleable.** A bonus the operator wouldn't actually charge for has no incentive value. The customer must believe they are trading the testimonial for real economic value.
- **A boring award produces no submissions.** Hormozi's instruction is unusually emphatic: *"don't do a boring thing."* Generic certificates and plaques don't move customers; community-symbolic objects do.
- **4-Proofs is structurally rigid.** Skipping the community post (Proof 1) loses scrapable written proof. Skipping the unboxing (Proof 3) loses the reaction asset. Skipping the on-stage (Proof 4) loses the public-recognition piece. Each is the asset the others can't substitute for.
- **Two-award production is non-optional.** A single shared trophy means the personalized version ships *or* the on-stage moment happens, not both. The 4-Proofs structure depends on having both versions.
- **Aspirational milestones must be reachable.** A milestone too far out produces zero redemptions. A milestone too easy makes the award status-free. Tune to roughly the top 5–15% of the customer base over a defined period.
- **Submissions need a manual review path.** Some submissions will not meet the quality bar. Define what happens: re-submit, partial credit, decline. Document up front to avoid disputes.
- **Compliance posture stays the same.** All submitted videos and posts must be authorized for advertising use. The submission instructions should include the consent line.
