# Retention — Book Home

## Parent
- [[../Hormozi-Home|Hormozi Home]]

## Purpose
Hub for the Hormozi playbook *$100M Playbook: Retention — How to Get Customers to Keep Buying* (also titled "Churn Checklist"). Holds distilled framework notes derived from this playbook. Retention is a tactical playbook centered on one operating thesis: **a business with a revolving door of customers is hardly a business at all** — and on a single composite framework (the Churn Checklist) made of nine retention levers any operator can run against any subscription, membership, or repeat-purchase business.

This playbook is the canonical home in the vault for churn-reduction mechanics. *$100M Playbook: Lifetime Value* (Crazy Eight, Lever 3, sub-mechanic "Decrease churn") explicitly punts the depth here. This hub does not contain raw book content. Provenance is carried via `source_reference` frontmatter on each canonical note plus the Source Map.

## Reuse vs. New Decisions
This playbook sits at the intersection of LTV, Money Models, Offers, and Fast Cash. It is the canonical home for retention/churn mechanics; it reuses upstream concepts without redefining them.

- **LTV / churn math (lifetime = price ÷ churn; 3.3x LTV from 10%→3% churn)** — defined in `04-100M-Lost-Chapters/`. Retention restates the formula in context. Canonical home stays there. See [[../04-100M-Lost-Chapters/Hormozi-CFA-Three-Levers|Hormozi — CFA Three Levers]].
- **Pricing Power / Value Equation** — defined in `01-100M-Offers/`. The Price–Value–Churn section in Retention applies these; it does not redefine them. See [[../01-100M-Offers/Hormozi-Pricing-Power|Pricing Power]] and [[../01-100M-Offers/Hormozi-Value-Equation|Value Equation]].
- **Price testing method (Conv × LTGP)** — defined in `09-Lifetime-Value/`. Retention's Price–Value–Churn table is consistent with this and should be tested using that procedure. See [[../09-Lifetime-Value/Hormozi-Price-Testing-Method|Hormozi — Price Testing Method]].
- **Crazy Eight (Lever 3 sub-mechanic "Decrease churn")** — defined in `09-Lifetime-Value/`. Crazy Eight explicitly delegates churn-reduction depth to this playbook. See [[../09-Lifetime-Value/Hormozi-Crazy-Eight|Hormozi — The Crazy Eight]].
- **Continuity / Recurring Offers** — defined in `03-100M-Money-Models/`. Retention's Annual Payment Options note links here for recurring-architecture context. See [[../03-100M-Money-Models/Hormozi-Continuity-Offers|Continuity Offers]].
- **Upsell Offers (used as save-with-upsell mechanism)** — defined in `03-100M-Money-Models/`. Cancellation Saves links here. See [[../03-100M-Money-Models/Hormozi-Upsell-Offers|Upsell Offers]].
- **Fast Cash Play** — defined in `06-Fast-Cash/`. Retention references this directly as an activation-point mechanism (the Gym Launch first-30-day ROI play that took churn from 8% → 3%). Linked from [[Hormozi-Activation-Points|Activation Points]]. See [[../06-Fast-Cash/Hormozi-Fast-Cash-Play|Fast Cash Play]].

This book contributes the entire retention canon: the Churn Checklist umbrella, the nine operational levers, the Five Horsemen predecessor, the Common Factors Method as a generalizable problem-solving meta-tool, the Value-Per-Second design principle, and the Activate–Testimonial–Refer–Ascend customer journey.
