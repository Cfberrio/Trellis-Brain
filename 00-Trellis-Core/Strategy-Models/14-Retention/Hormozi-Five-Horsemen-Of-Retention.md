# Hormozi — Five Horsemen of Retention

## Parent
- [[00-Book-Home|Retention — Book Home]]

## Purpose
Gym-era retention framework that preceded and informed the Churn Checklist. Five concrete activities that, run together, took Gym Launch portfolio churn from ~15% to ~3% MoM in three months. Documented separately from the [[Hormozi-Churn-Checklist|Churn Checklist]] because (a) it is the specific set of tactics for high-touch, in-person, recurring service businesses (gyms, studios, clinics, in-person memberships); and (b) it includes the **"shaking the three"** rollout pattern — the operator-facing expectation-setting curve that prevents teams from killing a working retention program in month one.

## Core Idea
> Five activities. Run them all. Expect churn to *increase* in month one ("shaking the three"), then halve in month two, then halve again in month three. Hold the line through the spike — it is the program working, not failing.

The Five Horsemen are the result of running the [[Hormozi-Common-Factors-Method|Common Factors Method]] across the subset of gym owners running <3% MoM churn for 6 consecutive months. They are the overlap, not the curated best-practice list. That is why they hold up.

## Framework / Structure

### The five activities

**1. Track attendance.**
Watch each member's session count week over week. The leading indicator pattern in low-churn gyms:

| Week | Sessions |
|---|---|
| 1 | 3 |
| 2 | 2 |
| 3 | 2 |
| 4 | 1 |
| 5 | CANCEL |

Decline rule: members who drop to **2 sessions/week** are headed to cancel. Reaching out in week 2 (not week 4) recovers them:

| Week | Sessions | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 3 | — |
| 2 | 2 | **Reach out** |
| 3 | 3 | (recovered) |
| 4 | 3 | — |
| 5 | 3 | — |

Translate to non-gym businesses by replacing "sessions/week" with the leading-indicator usage metric — see [[Hormozi-Activation-Points|Activation Points]] for the generalization.

**2. Reach out 2x per week.**
Praise participation and progress. Solve small problems. Both — not either-or. Frequency matters more than scripted depth.

**3. Handwritten cards.**
On signup, at referral asks (3 / 6 / 12 month milestones), holidays, birthdays. Hand-made specifically — printed cards do not produce the effect. Operator constraint: time. Operator return: outsized loyalty per minute spent.

**4. Member events.**
Hold regular events on an internal calendar. Cadence rule: **regular to you, random to them.** Hormozi's defaults: every 21, 42, or 63 days. Use handwritten invites (Horseman #3). Dual benefit: cuts churn (relational density — see [[Hormozi-Community-Linking|Community Linking]]) and produces referrals.

**5. Exit interviews.**
Cancellation triggers a required interview before billing stops. Set the expectation **at onboarding**, not at cancellation.
- Show rate: ~50%.
- Save rate within those who show: ~50%.
- Net churn cut: ~25%.
- LTV lift: ~33%.

Mechanism is operationalized at scale in [[Hormozi-Cancellation-Saves|Cancellation Saves]] (lever #7 of the Churn Checklist).

### "Shaking the three" — the rollout pattern
Implementing all five horsemen produces a counterintuitive month-one spike before the gains:

| Month | Churn | Δ |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 10% → **15%** | **+50%** ("shaking the three") |
| 2 | 15% → 7% | −53% |
| 3 | 7% → 3% | −57% |

Two reasons month one spikes:
1. **People who had already churned mentally** but were still being billed (auto-pay overhang). Increased contact reminds them they are still paying for something they stopped using; they cancel.
2. **People on the cancel-this-month edge** churn anyway; the activity does not save them, it just surfaces the loss earlier in the month.

> "Shaking the three" = both kinds shake out at once. A 9% → 3% MoM churn drop is a **3.3× LTV lift** (see [[Hormozi-Churn-Economics|Churn Economics]]).

## Strategic Implications
- **Run all five together.** The framework is the overlap of what works, not a menu. Operators who pick three of five reliably underperform operators who run all five.
- **Set the month-one expectation in advance.** Without warning the team and the owner about "shaking the three," the rollout dies in week three when the spike scares everyone. The expectation curve is part of the framework, not a footnote.
- **Generalize the pattern, not the tactic.** Outside gyms, "track attendance" becomes "track usage of the activation event" ([[Hormozi-Activation-Points|Activation Points]]); "reach out 2x/week" becomes [[Hormozi-Customer-Survey-ACA|ACA reach-outs]]; "member events" becomes [[Hormozi-Community-Linking|Community Linking]]; "exit interviews" becomes [[Hormozi-Cancellation-Saves|Cancellation Saves]]. The Churn Checklist is the generalization.
- **Handwritten cards punch above their weight per dollar.** They are nearly impossible to scale, which is the point — they are the unscalable component the standard offer cannot include. Same logic as [[../06-Fast-Cash/Hormozi-Unscalable-Value-Levers|Unscalable Value Levers]].
- **3% MoM churn is the operator's reasonable floor.** Hormozi: "I have yet to see a business with less than 3% churn that does not make great money."

## Practical Use
1. **Pick the leading-indicator metric** for your business (attendance for gyms; logins, posts, sessions, calls, completions for others). Set the threshold at which you reach out.
2. **Set the reach-out cadence at 2x/week** for high-touch businesses. Use a CRM or coach roster to make it a routine, not a heroic.
3. **Stand up a handwritten-card workflow** with pre-printed addresses + blank cards. Owner / coach writes at signup, milestones, holidays, birthdays.
4. **Calendar member events on a regular-to-you / random-to-them cadence.** Default 21 / 42 / 63 days. Handwritten invites.
5. **Add the cancellation-interview clause to the onboarding script** ("we require a quick interview before cancellation — set the expectation here so it is not friction later").
6. **Brief the team on "shaking the three"** before rollout. Plot the expected curve. Hold rollout discipline through month one.
7. **Re-measure churn at month 2 and month 3.** If the curve is not halving each month, audit the activity discipline first, not the framework.

## Notes / Limits
- **High-touch context.** The framework was developed for in-person memberships at ~$200–$500/month price points. For low-touch / low-price digital products, the [[Hormozi-Churn-Checklist|Churn Checklist's nine levers]] generalize the same logic — use those instead, and use the Five Horsemen as illustrative.
- **Reach-out content matters less than reach-out frequency.** Operators who script reach-outs over-index on words and under-index on consistency. The praise-and-problem-solve loop is robust to messy execution as long as it actually happens.
- **Exit interview save rate is bounded by category.** ~50% / ~50% is the gym number. SaaS recovery rates are lower; coaching/consulting recovery rates can be higher.
- **The Skool dataset added more.** This framework remains the in-person canon. The expanded set of nine levers in the Churn Checklist incorporates the Skool data that came after.
