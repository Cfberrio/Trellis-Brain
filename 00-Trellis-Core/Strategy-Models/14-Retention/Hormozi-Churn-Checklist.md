# Hormozi — Churn Checklist

## Parent
- [[00-Book-Home|Retention — Book Home]]

## Purpose
Umbrella framework of the *Retention* playbook. Decomposes "reduce churn" into **nine** discrete operational levers any operator can run against any subscription, membership, or repeat-purchase business. Replaces the gym-specific [[Hormozi-Five-Horsemen-Of-Retention|Five Horsemen]] with a generalized set tested across the Skool platform's many businesses. Designed as the standing checklist Acquisition.com walks every new portfolio company through.

## Core Idea
> "There is no magic bullet for churn. It's 100 tiny things that all save a few people each. And those people stack up into mountains of cash over time."

The Churn Checklist is not nine alternatives — it is nine compounding moves. No single lever is dominant. Each one saves a few customers. Run together, they take the typical recurring-revenue business from 8–15% MoM churn into the 3–5% range, which is the band where operations stabilize and the LTV math compounds.

## Framework / Structure

### The nine levers (in book order)

| # | Lever | Mechanism | Canonical Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Figure out activation points | Find the leading indicator of retention via top-20% common-factor analysis | [[Hormozi-Activation-Points\|Activation Points]] |
| 2 | Onboard customers | Teach every new customer to hit the activation point | [[Hormozi-Customer-Onboarding\|Customer Onboarding]] |
| 3 | Incentivize customer activation | Reward activation behavior with unlockables; place additional unlocks past major churn points | [[Hormozi-Activation-Incentives\|Activation Incentives]] |
| 4 | Community linking / events | Connect members to each other, not to you | [[Hormozi-Community-Linking\|Community Linking]] |
| 5 | Fire or correct bad customers | Pin best behavior; 3-strike rule; structured topic categories | [[Hormozi-Customer-Curation\|Customer Curation]] |
| 6 | Add annual pricing options | Annual billing rules; Big-Head-Long-Tail; founder rates | [[Hormozi-Annual-Payment-Options\|Annual Payment Options]] |
| 7 | Cancellation call / video | Required exit interview at cancellation; save-with-redo or save-with-upsell | [[Hormozi-Cancellation-Saves\|Cancellation Saves]] |
| 8 | Survey customers regularly | Twice-yearly keep/remove survey + ACA reach-outs | [[Hormozi-Customer-Survey-ACA\|Customer Survey & ACA]] |
| 9 | Four-step customer journey | Activate → Testimonial → Refer → Ascend | [[Hormozi-Customer-Journey-Milestones\|Customer Journey Milestones]] |

### How the levers compose
Architecture (read top-down):

```
Identify the right customer  ─►  Activation Points (#1)
                                  │
Drive every customer to it    ─►  Onboarding (#2)
                                  │
Reward staying active         ─►  Incentives (#3)
                                  │
Add relational stickiness     ─►  Community Linking (#4)
                                  │
Curate the cohort             ─►  Customer Curation (#5)
                                  │
Match payment to retention    ─►  Annual Payment Options (#6)
                                  │
Save the leavers              ─►  Cancellation Saves (#7)
                                  │
Listen continuously           ─►  Survey & ACA (#8)
                                  │
Plan the long arc             ─►  Customer Journey (#9)
```

Levers 1–3 are sequential (each one depends on the prior). Levers 4–9 are mostly independent and can be added in any order once the activation core is in place.

### Recommended implementation order
1. **First wave (must-do, sequenced):** #1 Activation Points → #2 Onboarding → #3 Incentives.
2. **Second wave (parallelizable):** #4 Community Linking, #5 Customer Curation, #7 Cancellation Saves.
3. **Third wave (structural changes):** #6 Annual Payment Options, #9 Customer Journey.
4. **Continuous:** #8 Customer Survey & ACA — runs always, twice-yearly survey + every-2–3-week reach-outs.

### Coverage rule
> "Not all of these will apply to your business. So if you find one and think 'That won't work for me' — you might be right — but many of them will."

Operate on coverage, not perfection. Pick the levers that fit; ship them; measure. Skipping a lever because it does not fit is fine; skipping a lever because it is unfamiliar is the trap.

## Strategic Implications
- **Compounding over single-lever optimization.** No single lever takes 15% churn to 3%. Six of them together do. Operators who keep searching for the one big retention move keep losing the cumulative gains from running the standing checklist.
- **The checklist is the install playbook.** Acquisition.com's first move with every new portfolio company is this checklist. Treat it as the diagnostic + operating system, not a brainstorm input.
- **Order matters at the top.** Without an identified activation point (#1), onboarding (#2) has no destination, and incentives (#3) reward the wrong behavior. Skipping the first three rarely works.
- **Structural moves (#6, #9) shift the LTV ceiling, not just the floor.** Annual billing and ascension change the unit economics, not just retention percentage. They are the levers that turn a saved business into a scaling one.
- **Retention is also referrals.** Every lever in the checklist also lifts referrals (happier customers refer more). With `referrals > churn`, the business compounds without paid acquisition (see [[Hormozi-Churn-Economics|Churn Economics]]).

## Practical Use
Operator checklist (synthesized from the Putting It All Together / tear-off summaries on pp. 31–32 and 34–35):

1. **Find your activation point.** Top 20% of churned-and-stayed-3+-months customers → common factors → 5 candidates → narrow to 1–2 by testing. ([[Hormozi-Activation-Points|details]])
2. **Update messaging** to attract people who match the top-20% profile. Update **paid ads, organic content, sales pages**.
3. **Update onboarding** to drive every new customer to the activation point. Add onboarding calls. Resell value during onboarding. Use names. Tell them about unlockables, community linking. Give homework with a 7-day deadline. Schedule the next call. ([[Hormozi-Customer-Onboarding|details]])
4. **Implement unlockables.** Course unlocks, 1-on-1 calls, virtual/live events, higher-tier calls, custom badges/profile images. Time additional unlocks past average lifespan, then 30 days, 3 months, 3 months. ([[Hormozi-Activation-Incentives|details]])
5. **Wire up community linking.** Manually introduce members, host group events, start a community podcast, elevate micro-celebrities. ([[Hormozi-Community-Linking|details]])
6. **Curate the cohort.** Pin best posts, praise in public; eliminate bad behavior with feedback + a 3-strike rule. Structure post topics into Wins / Fun / Discovered / Meetups. ([[Hormozi-Customer-Curation|details]])
7. **Add annual pricing options.** Annual billing option ("buy 10 get 2 free"). Try Big-Head-Long-Tail (1-time setup + small monthly) where appropriate. Founder rates as alternative. ([[Hormozi-Annual-Payment-Options|details]])
8. **Stand up the cancellation flow.** Tell new customers you require cancellation calls. Resell on staying. Loss-aversion close on what they will lose (data, posts, access, founder pricing). ([[Hormozi-Cancellation-Saves|details]])
9. **Make the product easy to consume.** Twice-yearly keep/remove survey. Every-2–3-week ACA reach-outs (Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask). ([[Hormozi-Customer-Survey-ACA|details]])
10. **Implement the four-milestone customer journey.** Activate → Testimonial → Refer → Ascend. Don't forget to sell them again. ([[Hormozi-Customer-Journey-Milestones|details]])

## Notes / Limits
- **The checklist is necessary, not sufficient.** Pricing, product fit, and a [[Hormozi-Churn-Definition|workable price–value equation]] sit upstream. The checklist multiplies a working business; it does not save a broken offer.
- **You will not know which lever did what.** Hormozi: "I don't know which steps worked better than others; I just know that doing them all worked." Operators who try to A/B-test single retention levers usually conclude nothing moved (per-lever effect is small) and abandon the program. Run the bundle.
- **Some levers cost money or capacity.** 1-on-1 onboarding, cancellation calls, manual community intros all consume operator/team time. Budget retention spend at 1/5 × CAC per customer (see [[Hormozi-Churn-Economics|Churn Economics]]).
- **Re-run #1 every 6–12 months.** Activation points drift as the customer base changes. The rest of the checklist should be reset against the new activation point on the same cadence.
