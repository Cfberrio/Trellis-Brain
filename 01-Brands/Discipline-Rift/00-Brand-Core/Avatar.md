## Primary avatar — parent payer
Parent of a child ages 6–12 at a participating school who wants their child active, confident, and socially connected, and prefers a low-friction, on-campus, beginner-friendly season over off-campus sports logistics.

### Parent segments
**Segment 1 — New-to-sports / anxious kid**
- They are buying psychological safety and belonging.
- Their questions revolve around beginners, shyness, embarrassment, and coach vibe.
- Their biggest fear is emotional discomfort, not price.

**Segment 2 — Logistics-overwhelmed parent**
- They are buying convenience and routine simplicity.
- Their questions revolve around pickup, aftercare, timing, and ease.
- Their biggest enemy is friction, not motivation.

**Segment 3 — Price-sensitive but willing if simple**
- They are buying predictable commitment and low regret.
- Their questions revolve around value, included items, hidden costs, and whether it is worth it.
- Their biggest fear is paying for something the child will abandon quickly.

## Secondary avatar — school gatekeeper
Principal, AP, enrichment lead, or administrator who wants more student participation and better after-school options without creating supervision, dismissal, communication, or staff workload problems.

### What schools fear
- dismissal chaos,
- safety incidents,
- front-office overload,
- unreliable vendors,
- parent complaints,
- programs that feel exclusive rather than inclusive.

### What schools want to hear
- structured supervision,
- defined dismissal flow,
- clear parent communication handled by DR,
- coach reliability,
- beginner-friendly and inclusive positioning,
- low lift for school staff.
