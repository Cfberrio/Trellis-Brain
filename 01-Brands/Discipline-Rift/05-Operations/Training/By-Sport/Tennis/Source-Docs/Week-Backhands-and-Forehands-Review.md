TENNIS SIMON SAYS
Setup:
Players stand on one side of the court along the baseline.
Feet are shoulder-width apart.
Dominant hand on the grip, secondary hand on the neck
of the racket.
Players start upright and relaxed, holding their rackets.
Game Rules:
The coach (acting as "Simon") gives commands, but
players should only follow if the command starts with 
"Simon says...".
If the coach gives a command without saying "Simon says"
and a player follows it, that player is eliminated.
Commands:
"Simon says, Ready!": Players quickly move into the ready
position (feet wide, knees bent, racket prepared).
"Simon says, Back!": Players return to their upright,
relaxed position.
"Simon says, Left!" or "Right!": Players shuffle laterally to
the left or right while maintaining the ready position.
"Simon says, Ball!": Coach simulates hitting a ball to one
side, and players move in that direction as if preparing to
hit it.
"Simon says, Split Step!": Players perform a small jump or 
split step, preparing for a shot.
"Simon says, Forehand!": Players move into forehand
position:
Turn the body toward the dominant hand side.

BACKHANDS (30 MIN)
FORM
Start in the Ready Position: Feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent,
racquet held with both hands, facing the net.
Turn Foot Positioning to the Secondary Handʼs Side: Rotate your body so
that your non-dominant hand leads the motion.
Grip: Place both hands on the handle of the racquet, ensuring proper grip
Eastern or Semi-Western for two-handed, Continental for one-handed).
Racquet Alignment: Align the racquet with your body, keeping it close to
your torso.
Swing: Use a low-to-high motion to swing the racquet towards the net,
aiming to make contact with the ball around waist height.
Follow Through: Finish the swing above the opposite shoulder to ensure
proper mechanics.
Return to Ready Position: After the swing, return to the ready position to
prepare for the next shot.

PLAYER CUES
BEGINNER/INTERMEDIATE
Racquet too far back: Ensure players donʼt overextend their
racquet behind their body.
Improper grip: Check that players are placing their hands on
the racquet handle correctly.
Moving incorrectly towards the ball: Emphasize staying
balanced and swinging properly without lunging towards the
ball.
Not finishing through the shot: Ensure players finish with the
racquet above the opposite shoulder to complete the stroke.

Brushing the Ball: Emphasize that players need to brush the
ball with their racquet using a low-to-high swing path to
generate topspin.

Brushing Too Much:

HANGMAN
Setup:
Line players up behind the baseline on one side of the
court.
Players will take turns hitting the skill of the day (e.g.,
backhand) or previously learned skills.
Game Rules:
Objective: Players must hit the ball over the net and within
the court boundaries.
Body Parts: If a player succeeds, they "lose" a body part
(start with one arm, then a leg, etc.). The progression
continues until they are hitting from the ground.
If a player misses their last attempt (after all body parts are
"lost"), they go to the other side of the net, also known as 
jail (or become a shark or go into detention).
Escaping Jail:
To escape from jail, players must catch a ball, either after 
one bounce or in the air.
Winning Condition:
Players continue until only one player remains, or the coach
decides to end the game. Those who avoid missing shots
and "losing body parts" will keep going.

KING / QUEEN OF THE COURT ADVANCED
1. Set Up:
Choose one or two players to be the king/queen on one side
of the net.
The rest of the players line up on the opposite side of the
court.
2. Challenger Rotation:
Players from the opposite side challenge the king/queen one at
a time by playing a point against them.
3. Winning Conditions:
First Point: If the challenger wins the first point, they earn the
right to play a second point against the king/queen.
Second Point: If the challenger wins two consecutive points,
they become the new king/queen.
4. Automatic Victory:
If a challenger hits a winner (a shot that the king/queen cannot
reach or return) during the first point, they immediately
become the new king/queen, skipping the need for a second
point.
5. Repeat:
Continue the drill, with challengers rotating through the line to
take on the reigning king/queen.
The game keeps going until a new king/queen is crowned or
the drill time ends.
