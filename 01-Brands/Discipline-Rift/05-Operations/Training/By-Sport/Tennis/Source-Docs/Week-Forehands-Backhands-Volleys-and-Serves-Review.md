THE LINES GAME
Court Overview: 
Begin by explaining the various lines on the tennis court
Instructions:

lines (e.g., "Baseline," "Service Line," or "Sideline").
Race to the Line:

Elimination:
The last player to reach the line is eliminated from the
round.
Practice Rounds:
Conduct a few practice rounds without eliminating players,
ensuring everyone understands the game.
Elimination Rounds:
After the practice rounds, start eliminating the last player to
reach the line until one player remains.

TENNIS SIMON SAYS
Setup:
Players stand on one side of the court along the baseline.
Feet are shoulder-width apart.
Dominant hand on the grip, secondary hand on the neck
of the racket.
Players start upright and relaxed, holding their rackets.
Game Rules:
The coach (acting as "Simon") gives commands, but
players should only follow if the command starts with 
"Simon says...".
If the coach gives a command without saying "Simon says"
and a player follows it, that player is eliminated.
Commands:
"Simon says, Ready!": Players quickly move into the ready
position (feet wide, knees bent, racket prepared).
"Simon says, Back!": Players return to their upright,
relaxed position.
"Simon says, Left!" or "Right!": Players shuffle laterally to
the left or right while maintaining the ready position.
"Simon says, Ball!": Coach simulates hitting a ball to one
side, and players move in that direction as if preparing to
hit it.
"Simon says, Split Step!": Players perform a small jump or 
split step, preparing for a shot.
"Simon says, Forehand!": Players move into forehand
position:
Turn the body toward the dominant hand side.

SKITTLES
Setup:
Players line up in a single horizontal line along the baseline.
The coach stands in the middle of the court with a supply
of tennis balls.
Gameplay:
The coach calls out different candy names (e.g.,
"Sneakers," "Starburst," "M&Mʼs").
When the coach says "Skittles," all players must run across
the court to the opposite baseline.
As they run, the coach tosses tennis balls towards the
players. The goal for the players is to dodge the balls.
Elimination:
If a player is touched by a ball, they are out and join the
coach in the middle.
Eliminated players can help the coach by calling out the
candy names but cannot toss the balls for safety reasons.
Winning:
The game continues until only one player remains
untagged. This player is the winner.
Purpose: 
Improve players' agility, reaction time, and dodging skills in
a fun and engaging way.

TENNIS TAG
Explain:
Define the boundaries on the court within which the game
will be played.
Select one player to start as "it," holding a tennis ball.
Gameplay:
The player who is "it" must tag other players by touching
them with the volleyball (no throwing, just a soft touch).
Players must stay within the boundaries; if anyone steps
out, they become "it."
The tagged player then becomes "it" and continues the
game.
Team Variation:
If there are many players, divide them into two teams:
Chasers: A team of players with volleyballs who try to
tag the runners.
Runners: The other team, without volleyballs, must
avoid being tagged.
The chasers have a set time (e.g., 30 seconds) to tag as
many runners as possible. After time is up, teams switch
roles.
Purpose: 
Develop agility, quickness, and spatial awareness while
adding an element of fun and teamwork.

KING / QUEEN OF THE COURT WITH SERVES
1. Set Up:
Choose one or two players to be the king/queen on one side
of the net.
The rest of the players line up on the opposite side of the
court.
2. Challenger Rotation:
Players from the opposite side challenge the king/queen one at
a time by playing a point against them.
3. Winning Conditions:
First Point: If the challenger wins the first point, they earn the
right to play a second point against the king/queen.
Second Point: If the challenger wins two consecutive points,
they become the new king/queen.
4. Automatic Victory:
If a challenger hits a winner (a shot that the king/queen cannot
reach or return) during the first point, they immediately
become the new king/queen, skipping the need for a second
point.
5. Repeat:
Continue the drill, with challengers rotating through the line to
take on the reigning king/queen.
The game keeps going until a new king/queen is crowned or
the drill time ends.

HANGMAN
Setup:
Line players up behind the baseline on one side of the
court.
Players will take turns hitting the skill of the day (e.g.,
backhand) or previously learned skills.
Game Rules:
Objective: Players must hit the ball over the net and within
the court boundaries.
Body Parts: If a player succeeds, they "lose" a body part
(start with one arm, then a leg, etc.). The progression
continues until they are hitting from the ground.
If a player misses their last attempt (after all body parts are
"lost"), they go to the other side of the net, also known as 
jail (or become a shark or go into detention).
Escaping Jail:
To escape from jail, players must catch a ball, either after 
one bounce or in the air.
Winning Condition:
Players continue until only one player remains, or the coach
decides to end the game. Those who avoid missing shots
and "losing body parts" will keep going.

BATTLESHIP
Setup:
Divide players into two teams.
Each team positions themselves on opposite sides of the
net.
Cone Placement:
Each team sets up cones (representing ships) on their side
of the court, strategically placing them in various locations.
Game Rules:
Players will take turns using the skill of the day (e.g.,
forehand, backhand) to try and hit the cones on the
opposite side of the net.
The goal is to hit and "sink" the opposing teamʼs ships
(cones) by knocking them down.
Winning Condition:
The first team to destroy all of the other teamʼs ships
(cones) wins the game.

JAILBREAK
Setup:
Line players up behind the baseline on one side of the
court.
Players take turns hitting the skill of the day (or previously
learned skills if it's past Week 1.
Game Rules:
Objective: Players aim to hit the ball over the net and into
the court.
Losing Lives: If successful, the player loses a life (starting
with a body part).
If they miss their shot, they go to the other side of the net
(jail, become a shark, or detention).
Escaping Jail:
To escape from jail, players must catch a ball from a rally
after one bounce or in the air.
Winning Condition:
Players continue hitting until only one player remains, or
until the coach ends the game. Players who successfully
keep the ball in play without missing stay in the game
longer.
Purpose:
This game helps players practice their consistency and
recovery while keeping things fun and competitive.

FIREBALL
Setup:
Players spread out across the court, ready for the coach to
hit balls at random spots.
Game Rules:
The coach will hit fewer balls than the number of players to
random locations on the court.
Players must quickly move to catch or hit the balls.
Any player who fails to get a ball will be eliminated and
must step aside to help the coach toss balls for the
remaining players.
Progression:
Each round, the number of balls decreases, ensuring that
there are always fewer balls than players.
Winning Condition:
The game continues until only one player remains, who is
declared the winner.
Purpose:
The purpose of this game is to enhance players' speed, 
anticipation, and court coverage.

RALLY ADVANCED
Set Up:
Choose one player to be on one side of the net.
The rest of the players line up on the opposite side.
Challenger Rotation:
Players take turns rallying with the player on the other side
of the net, one at a time.
Objective:
Each player attempts to keep the rally going for as long as
possible with the player on the opposite side.
Winning Condition:
The player who manages to sustain the rally for the longest
number of hits wins the round.
Repeat:
Continue the drill, rotating through the line of challengers.
Players can switch sides after each rally.

POPUP VOLLEYS
Instructions:
Player Setup:
Have players squat in a ready position close to the net,
facing the coach. Each player is assigned a number or a
name.
Execution:
The coach randomly calls out a playerʼs name or number.
The called player must "pop up" from the squatting
position to quickly execute either a forehand or backhand
volley fed by the coach.
After hitting the volley, the player returns to their squatting
position.
Format Options:
Beginner Level:
Use slower, more controlled feeds to allow players to
adjust and focus on form.
Call out names/numbers at a slower pace to give
players time to react.
Advanced Level:
Increase the speed of feeds and call out
names/numbers faster to improve reaction time.
Occasionally feed balls to players' non-dominant side
to challenge their ability to adjust.
Footwork:
Emphasize quick, explosive movement when popping up
from the squatting position.
