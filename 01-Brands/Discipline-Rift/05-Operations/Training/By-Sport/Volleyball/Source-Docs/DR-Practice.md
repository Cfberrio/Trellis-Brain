DR PRACTICE

Discipline Rift Universal Practice Structure
Elementary | Mixed experience | Any sport
Program purpose (Discipline Rift, For ALL Sports)
Why we coach: Sport is the vehicle; the outcomes are bigger than the scoreboard.
Belonging: every child is seen, safe, and has a role.
Effort Norms: we practice habits—listening fast, trying again, helping
teammates.
Psychological Safety + Productive Struggle: mistakes are expected; we coach
the next rep.
Learning Before Winning: we measure growth through Observable Evidence,
not hype.
Coach as Teacher: coaches create Durable Learning through smart Practice
Design and consistent CFU.

Tier System (solves mixed experience without splitting the group)
Same activity, different demands—athletes move tiers by Observable Evidence:
Tier 1: stable reps (blocked)
Tier 3: predictable sequence (serial)
Tier 5: same skill under a simple constraint (more Desired Difficulty)
Micro-CFU "Name the cue + show one rep."
Block 5 — Teaching Acquisition (Guided learning
under decisions) (10–12 min)
Purpose: connect technique to Decision-Making using See →  Decide →  Act.
This is where you train Perception, Perceptual Cues, Decision Speed, and
Decision Quality.
Design principles:
Representative Practice: looks like the game without full chaos
Desired Difficulty: add one variable (space / time / pressure), not three at once
Practice emphasis: I̲n̲t̲e̲r̲l̲e̲a̲v̲i̲n̲g̲  + light R̲a̲n̲d̲o̲m̲ i̲z̲e̲d̲ ̲P̲r̲a̲c̲t̲i̲c̲e̲
mix two related skills/decisions
vary the situation so kids must read and choose
Fast teaching moves (keep reps flowing):
Perception Question ("What did you notice?")
Freeze (rare)
Recreate the Situation
Replay immediately
Micro-CFU: athlete answers a perception question and points to evidence (stops
the Assumption Trap)

"Maximize every minute" operating rules
(coach standards)
One Learning Target per block.
One Cue per block (avoid Overloading).
Micro-CFU every 35 minutes using Observable Evidence.
Only 12 total Freeze moments per practice; always Replay after correction.
Protect Time-on-Task: transitions are taught like skills.
After practice: one 1% Solution (tiny fix) to improve next time Marginal Gains).
