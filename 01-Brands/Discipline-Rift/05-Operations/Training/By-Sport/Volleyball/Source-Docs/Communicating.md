COMMUNICATING

The focus is building comfort, habit, and shared language—starting with coach-
led call-and-response, then progressing into player-to-player communication.
Every coach uses the same call phrases so players learn to respond 
automatically and without hesitation.
Teaching Philosophy
Beginner players:
feel awkward speaking
donʼt know what to say
stay silent to avoid being wrong
Because of this, we teach communication through:
group responses first (low pressure)
short, simple words
repetition

Communication cues are actions, not conversations.
Core Concept: Communication Is a Habit Before Itʼs a
Skill
Before athletes can communicate in play, they must get comfortable making
sound with teammates.
We progress communication in this order:
Coach →  Team response
Player →  Player names
In-play responsibility calls
This order is non-negotiable.

Communication 2 — Call-and-Response Trigger Word
Cue: "Volley!"
Coach says:
"VOLLEY!"
Team responds (standardized):
"ATTENTION!"
If your program uses "Attention, volley!" that is fine. The wording must stay

This creates an instant, predictable lock-in moment that refocuses the group
immediately.
Communication 3 — Energy Phrase (Short + Unified)
Cue: "Ball!"
Sequence:
Coach: "VOLLEY!"
Team: "ATTENTION!"
Coach: "BALL!"
Team: "BALL!"
Why:
Short words build voice without needing volleyball knowledge.
Players learn to respond without thinking.

Communication 4 — Name Loud (Comfort Builder)
Cue: "Name!"
Coach instruction:
"Say one teammateʼs name out loud. Go."
Then wait until names are spoken.
Ways to prompt:
Coach points: "Say her name." (wait)
"One, two, three—NAME!" (players say names)
Why:
This bridges group speaking into individual speaking with very low pressure.
Key Coaching Moves (Non-Negotiable)
1) Wait for them to speak
If you talk over silence, you teach players that silence is acceptable.
Coach rule:
Give the cue
Pause
Do not continue until you hear the response
2) Correct silence like a skill error
Silence is not personality.
It is a habit—and habits are trained.
Treat silence the same way you treat:
poor posture
late platform
wrong footwork

3) Keep the words identical across coaches
Confidence comes from predictability.
If the language changes, hesitation increases.
How Communication Progresses Over Time
The goal is to move from:
group voice →  individual voice →  in-play voice
Early Phase (Week 1 Focus)
Goal: Break silence and normalize speaking
Primary communication used:
"Mine on three"
"Volley →  Attention"
"Ball"
teammate names
Coaching emphasis:
repetition
waiting for responses
praise for participation (not volume perfection)
Application Phase (Later Weeks)
Goal: Transfer voice into volleyball actions
Now we begin adding simple in-play language:
"Mine"
"Help"
"Free"
"Up"

"Next"
Coach-led triggers remain in use to maintain team voice and energy.
Non-Negotiables at All Levels
These never change:
Everyone responds to the coach
Everyone says something every practice
Coach waits for the response
Short words only
Same language across all coaches
Coach Reminder
If communication is weak, ask yourself:
Did I give them exact words?
Did I wait for the response?
Did I require names out loud?
Fix one expectation at a time.
Recommended Coach Script (Standardized)
"Letʼs communicate."
