MOVING

This module defines how we teach movement to beginners.
The focus is not speed or conditioning.
The focus is teaching players how to move efficiently, stay balanced, and arrive
ready to play the ball.
Movement is the foundation skill that connects:
passing
setting
defending
attacking
If players cannot move well, they cannot execute skills well.
Every coach teaches movement using the same language, so players move with
confidence instead of guessing.
Teaching Philosophy

### Page 2

Beginner players:
Reach instead of moving
Take too many steps or the wrong steps
Stand up while moving
Move late because they donʼt trust their feet
Because of this, we teach movement as:
A standalone skill
Built from simple patterns
Taught with clear names and cues
Corrected one piece at a time
Cues are corrections, not explanations.
Core Concept: Move First, Then Play the Ball
Movement always comes before technique.
We want players to learn:
when one step is enough
when they need to shuffle
how to move without standing up
how to arrive balanced and ready
MOVEMENT SKILLS BY NUMBERS (FOUNDATION)
These movement skills are used in every volleyball action.
Skill 1 — Step
Cue: "Step"
One quick step toward the ball

### Page 3

Why:

Skill 2 — Step–Shuffle
Cue: "Step–Shuffle"
One initiating step
One shuffle to finish
Body stays square and low
Used when:
The ball is farther but still controlled.
Why:
This covers more space while keeping balance.
Skill 3 — Step–Shuffle–Shuffle
Cue: "Step–Shuffle–Shuffle"
One step to start
Two quick shuffles
Stay low the entire time
Used when:
The ball is well outside the body or into a seam.
Why:
Players learn to travel distance without crossing feet or standing up.
Core Movement Rules (Non-Negotiable)
Feet move before arms

MOVEMENT AND BALANCE
Ready Position (Always First)
Cue: "Ready"
Knees bent
Chest tall
Weight on the balls of the feet
Why:
Good movement starts from good posture.
Stop to Play the Ball
Cue: "Stop"
Feet stop before contact when possible
Body is balanced
Why:
You cannot control the ball if your body is still moving.
Emergency movement comes later.)
MOVEMENT AND COURT AWARENESS
Movement is how players learn space.
By moving repeatedly from the same spot, players learn:
how much space they cover

HOW MOVEMENT SHOWS UP IN OTHER SKILLS
Passing: Step / Step–Shuffle before platform
Setting: Move early →  Stop →  Push up
Defense: Step / Shuffle within area
Attacking: Approach creates timing
Movement language stays the same across all skills.
HOW MOVEMENT PROGRESSES OVER TIME
The movement patterns never change.
Only speed, distance, and pressure increase.
We do not add complexity early.
Early Phase (Week 1 Focus)

Teaching emphasis:

Application Phase (Later Weeks)
Goal: Faster, more confident movement

Non-Negotiables at All Levels
These never change:
Ready before moving
Feet move first
Step before shuffle

Coaching Priorities to Remember
Movement is a skill, not a warm-up
Feet solve most problems
Over-moving is as harmful as under-moving
Teach space through repetition, not lectures
Cues are corrections, not explanations

Coach Reminder
If a skill breaks down, ask yourself movement first:
Did they move early?
Did they choose the right movement (step vs shuffle)?
Did they arrive balanced?
Fix movement before technique.
