SERVING

Developmental Volleyball)
Purpose of This Module
This module defines how we teach serving to beginners.
The focus is not power or aces.
The focus is building repeatable contact, directional control, and confidence
from the service line.
We teach underhand serving using an open palm technique so players can:
make clean contact
send the ball forward and up
succeed early and often
Every coach teaches serving the same way, using the same language, so players
build confidence instead of fear.

### Page 2

Teaching Philosophy
Beginner players:
Are intimidated by serving
Over-swing when told to "hit harder"
Struggle to control the ball with a closed fist
Because of this, we teach serving through:
A simple sequence
Clear body positioning
An open palm contact surface
One correction at a time
Cues are corrections, not explanations.
Curriculum Reference (Coach Alignment)
The open-palm underhand serve is a standard beginner technique supported in
foundational volleyball texts
Core Concept: Serving Is a Controlled Swing
Serving is not a throw.

### Page 3

Serving is not a punch.
Serving is a controlled forward swing that sends the ball:
forward
over the net
in bounds
Power comes from body position and swing path, not brute force.
UNDERHAND SERVE BY NUMBERS (FOUNDATION)
Position 1 — Ready
Cue: "Ready"
Feet staggered (opposite foot forward)
Knees slightly bent
Shoulders square to target
Ball held out in front of the body
Why:
A staggered stance gives balance and direction without over-rotation.
Position 2 — Present the Ball
Cue: "Hold"
Non-dominant hand holds the ball still
Ball is held below the waist
Arm is straight and relaxed
Important clarification:
The ball is not tossed.
Why:
A still ball creates predictable contact for beginners.

### Page 4

Position 3 — Swing with Open Palm
Cue: "Open Palm"
Serving arm swings straight back →  straight forward
Hand is open and firm
Fingers together
Palm faces the target
Contact point:
Center of the ball
Below the waist
Why:
An open palm:
increases surface area
reduces mishits
improves directional control
prevents punching or scooping habits
Position 4 — Finish to Target
Cue: "Finish"
Arm continues forward after contact
Hand finishes pointing at the target
Weight shifts forward naturally
Why:
A forward finish ensures the ball travels over the net, not straight up or into the
floor.
Core Serving Pattern
1 →  2 →  3 →  4

### Page 5

Ready →  Hold →  Open Palm →  Finish
This sequence is non-negotiable.
Key Teaching Cues (Coach Logic)
"Open Palm"
Used when:
players make a fist
contact sounds like a punch
ball flies sideways or backward
Reminds players:
flat, firm contact surface
controlled swing
"Swing Forward"
Used when:
players scoop the ball upward
ball goes straight up
arm stops at contact
Reinforces:
forward swing path
follow-through
"Finish"
Used when:
serves die short
arm stops too early

HOW SERVING PROGRESSES OVER TIME
The contact surface stays the same.
The swing stays the same.
Only these things change:
distance from the net
consistency of contact
accuracy to zones
We do not change technique to chase power.
Early Phase (Week 1 Focus)
Goal: Success and confidence
Teaching emphasis:
open palm contact
forward swing
ball going over the net
How coaches should teach:
short distance if needed
high repetition
celebrate makes, not speed
Primary cues:
"Ready"
"Hold"

Application Phase (Later Weeks)
Goal: Direction and consistency

Technique does not change.
Non-Negotiables at All Levels
These never change:
Open palm contact (no fist)
Ball held still (no toss)
Forward swing path
Finish toward the target
Same Ready →  Hold →  Open Palm →  Finish sequence
Coaching Priorities to Remember
Success builds confidence
Control matters more than power
Open palm beats a fist for beginners
Cues are corrections, not explanations
Same language across all coaches

Coach Reminder
If a serve is off, ask yourself:
Was the ball held still?
Was the contact made with an open palm?
Did the arm swing forward and finish?
Fix one thing at a time.
