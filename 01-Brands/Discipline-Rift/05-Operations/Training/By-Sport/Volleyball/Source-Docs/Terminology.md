TERMINOLOGY

I. Learning & Decision-Making Terms
Decision-Making
The ability to choose the correct action under time, space, and pressure
constraints.
Perception
What the athlete notices before acting; the foundation of good decisions.
See →  Decide →  Act
The learning chain: perception drives decisions, decisions drive actions.
Expertise Is in the Eyes
High performers differ by what they notice, not just what they can do.
Cues
What we want the players to learn
Perceptual Cues

Decision Speed
How quickly an athlete can perceive, decide, and act correctly.
Decision Quality
How often an athlete chooses the most effective option available.
Frameworks
Named patterns or concepts that help athletes organize decisions (e.g., spacing

Transfer

II. Practice Design & Planning Terms
Practice with Purpose
Designing practice intentionally to produce learning, not just activity.
Practice Design
The structure and sequencing of activities inside a single practice.
Season Design
Planning learning over multiple weeks to support long-term memory.
Representative Practice
Practice that reflects key elements of the game without full simulation.
Desired Difficulty
Rules or conditions added to force enough but not too much challenge.
Time-on-Task
The percentage of practice time athletes are actively engaged in learning reps.
Marginal Gains
Small, consistent improvements that compound over time.
1% Solutions

III. Practice Sequencing & Repetition Styles
Simple →  Complex Progression
Starting with clarity and adding realism only after understanding.
Blocked Practice
Repeating the same skill in a stable context.
Serial Practice
Practicing related skills in a predictable sequence.
Randomized Practice
Mixing skills or decisions unpredictably to improve transfer.
Retrieval Practice
Asking athletes to recall learning from memory, not re-teaching it.
Spaced Practice
Revisiting learning over time instead of massing it all at once.
Interleaving
Mixing different skills or decisions within the same session or unit.
IV. Feedback & Questioning Terms
Feedback
Information given to help athletes improve future performance.
Specific Feedback
Feedback that names the exact behavior, cue, or decision.
Actionable Feedback
Feedback that tells the athlete what to do differently next rep.
Timely Feedback
Feedback given when athletes can immediately apply it.

Freeze

V. Checking for Understanding (CFU) Terms
CFU Checking for Understanding)
Methods used to verify whether athletes actually learned.
Observable Evidence
Visible behaviors that show learning has occurred.
Micro-CFU
Very short checks 530 seconds) embedded inside practice.
Exit Criteria
Clear, observable standards for demonstrating learning.
I Taught It vs They Learned It
The distinction between instruction delivered and learning achieved.
Assumption Trap
Believing learning happened without evidence.

Working Memory
The limited mental space athletes use while learning.
Cognitive Load
The total mental effort required during a task.
Overloading
Giving too much information, reducing learning.
Attention Management
Designing practice so attention stays on the learning target.
Long-Term Memory
Learning that persists and is retrievable later.
Durable Learning
Learning that holds up under pressure and time.
VII. Culture & Motivation Terms
Belonging
Athletes feeling seen, safe, and valued in the environment.
Effort Norms
Clear expectations for hustle, focus, and persistence.
Psychological Safety
An environment where mistakes are part of learning.
Productive Struggle
Allowing athletes to work through difficulty without rescuing too early.
Learning Before Winning
Prioritizing development to produce better performance over time.
Relationships Through Teaching
Building trust by helping athletes improve, not just encouraging them.

VIII. Coach Behavior & Identity Terms
Coach as Teacher
Viewing coaching as a teaching craft, not just leadership or motivation.
Technical Coaching
Attention to small, specific teaching moves.
Humble
A mindset of constant refinement and learning as a coach.
Toolbox Mentality
Using multiple strategies instead of a single system.
IX. Programmatic / System Terms (Derived Usage)
These are Lemov-aligned terms adapted for curriculum systems)
Tier System
A progression system based on observable learning, not age alone.
Learning Target
The specific skill or decision practice is designed to improve.
Practice Structure
The repeatable structure of a practice session.
Constraint Game
A game designed to force learning behaviors.
Culture Routine
A repeated action that reinforces norms automatically.
Coach Script
Pre-planned language used for cues, corrections, and transitions.
Metrics That Matter
Measurements tied to learning, not just outcomes.
