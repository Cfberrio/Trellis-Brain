SETTING

Developmental Volleyball)
Purpose of This Module
This module defines how we teach setting to beginners.
The focus is not perfect location or speed.
The focus is building repeatable habits that create consistent sets:
Feet move early
Body stops under the ball
Legs push straight up
Fingertips guide the ball
Every coach teaches setting the same way, using the same language, so players
build confidence and consistency.

### Page 2

Teaching Philosophy
Beginner players:
Panic when the ball is above their face
Try to "throw" with their arms
Lose control when their feet keep moving at contact
Because of this, we teach setting through:
A numbered sequence
Short, imagery-based cues
One correction at a time
Cues are corrections, not explanations.
Core Concept: Setting Is Legs First
Setting is not a hand skill first.
Setting is a whole-body lift.
The legs create the set. The fingers guide it.
If the ball doesnʼt go up, itʼs almost always because:
they didnʼt get stopped, or
they didnʼt push up with legs
SETTING BY NUMBERS (FOUNDATION)
Position 1 — Ready
Cue: "Ready"
Athletic stance
Knees bent (already loaded)
Chest tall
Hands relaxed near chest/face

### Page 3

Ready to move
Important clarification:
Hands stay relaxed until the player has moved under the ball.
Why:
Beginners must move first. Early hand-setting posture makes them stiff and late.
Position 2 — Under & Stop
Cue: "Beat it. Stop."
Move feet early to get under the ball
SETTING
3


### Page 4

Stop your feet before contact
Balanced base (no drifting)
Why:
You canʼt push the ball up if your body is still moving sideways/forward.
Stopping creates a base for a vertical leg push.
Position 3 — Window / Hand Shape
Cue: "Hershey Kiss"
Hands rise above the forehead
Hands form a Hershey Kiss shape (rounded and soft)
Fingers spread comfortably
Thumbs and index fingers create the "window"
Elbows slightly bent
Why:
This shape prepares the hands for clean fingertip contact after the body is set.

### Page 5

Position 4 — Finger Tips + Lift
Cue: "Finger Tips… Up!"
Ball contacts finger pads / fingertips
No palm contact
Legs extend straight up (knees + hips)
Finish tall

### Page 6

Fingers guide the release upward
Important clarification:
This is not a hold or catch.
It is a quick fingertip contact timed with a vertical leg push.
Why:
Legs provide lift (height and distance)
Fingertips provide control (direction and clean release)

Core Setting Pattern
1 →  2 →  3 →  4
Ready →  Under & Stop →  Hershey Kiss →  Finger Tips + Up
This sequence is non-negotiable.

### Page 8

"Beat it. Stop."
Used when:
players drift into contact
sets go forward/flat
contact looks like a push/throw
Creates:
balance under the ball
vertical body position
better fingertip consistency
"Hershey Kiss"
Used when:
hands are flat or stiff
fingers are tight together
contact looks like a slap
Creates:
soft, repeatable hand shape
readiness for fingertip contact
"Finger Tips"
Used when:
ball hits palms
ball sounds like a slap
ball spins wildly
Reminds players:
fingertips control the ball

HOW SETTING PROGRESSES OVER TIME
The contact never changes.
Only the movement before the set changes.
But the rule stays the same:
Move early →  Stop →  Push up.
Early Phase (Week 1 Focus)
Goal: Vertical lift + fingertip control
Teaching emphasis:
knees bent before contact
stopping under the ball
legs pushing straight up
fingertip contact above the forehead
How coaches should teach:
controlled tosses/self-toss
slow, repeatable reps

Goal: Setting while moving, still vertical

Non-Negotiables at All Levels
These never change:
Move first, hands second
Stop your feet before setting
Legs push straight up (not forward)
Contact above the forehead
Hershey Kiss hand shape
Fingertips/finger pads contact (no palms)
Fingers guide what the legs create
Coaching Priorities to Remember
Legs create lift
Balance comes before direction
Fingertips control the ball

### Page 12

Palms do not set

Same language across all coaches
Coach Reminder
If a set is off, ask yourself in this order:
Did they move under the ball early?
Did they stop their feet before contact?
Did their legs push straight up and finish tall?
Did the ball contact fingertips (not palms)?
Fix one thing at a time.
