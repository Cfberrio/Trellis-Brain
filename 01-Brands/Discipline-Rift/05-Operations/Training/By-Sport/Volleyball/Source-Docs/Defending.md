DEFENDING

Developmental Volleyball)
Purpose of This Module
This module defines how we teach defense to beginners.
The focus is not reading hitters or running defensive systems.
The focus is teaching players to:
Stay ready
Move efficiently within their area
Get their body behind the ball
Keep the ball off the floor
Defense is also where players begin to understand:
where they are on the court
what space they are responsible for
basic volleyball terms like front row, back row, positions, and rotations

### Page 2

Every coach teaches defense using the same movement language, so players
build court awareness through repetition.
Teaching Philosophy
Beginner players:
Freeze when the ball is attacked
Reach instead of moving
Do not yet understand their area of coverage
Because of this, we teach defense through:
Intentional movement patterns
Simple, repeatable cues
One correction at a time
Position-based repetition (learning space through movement, not lecture)
Cues are corrections, not explanations.
Core Concept: Defense Is Movement First
Defense is not reaction with the arms.
Defense is movement with the feet.
Players must learn:
how far to move
how fast to move
when one step is enough
when multiple shuffles are required
We do this by teaching three defensive movement skills.
DEFENSIVE MOVEMENT SKILLS (FOUNDATION)
These are taught explicitly and used every day in defense.

### Page 3

Skill 1 — Step
Cue: "Step"
One quick step to the ball
Used for balls just outside the body
Why:
Most defensive balls do not require big movement.
Over-moving creates imbalance.
Skill 2 — Step–Shuffle
Cue: "Step–Shuffle"
One step to start
One shuffle to finish
Used for balls farther away but still controlled
Why:
This keeps the body square and low while covering more space.
Skill 3 — Step–Shuffle–Shuffle
Cue: "Step–Shuffle–Shuffle"
One initiating step
Two quick shuffles
Used for balls well outside the body or into seams
Why:
Players must learn how to travel distance without standing up or crossing feet.
Core Concept: Move Within Your Area
We do not teach players to chase every ball.
We teach them to:

### Page 4

move within their area
use the right movement skill to cover that space
Area awareness is learned by repeating movement from the same position.
DEFENSE BY NUMBERS (FOUNDATION)
Position 1 — Ready
Cue: "Ready"
Athletic stance
Knees bent
Chest tall
Weight on the balls of the feet
Hands in front
Why:
A good ready position allows immediate stepping or shuffling.
Position 2 — Read
Cue: "Read"
Eyes track the ball
See where it is going
Why:
Movement choice depends on reading the ball early.
Position 3 — Move
Cue: "Step / Step–Shuffle / Step–Shuffle–Shuffle"
Choose the smallest movement needed
Stay low

### Page 5

Feet move before platform forms
Why:
Feet determine success in defense.
Position 4 — Platform
Cue: "Platform"
Same platform as passing
Arms flat and quiet
Built after movement
Why:
Defense does not use a different platform.
Position 5 — Up
Cue: "Up"
Legs extend
Ball goes up and playable
Why:
A good defensive play keeps the rally alive.
Core Defensive Pattern
1 →  2 →  3 →  4 →  5
Ready →  Read →  Move →  Platform →  Up
This sequence is non-negotiable.

### Page 6

Players should practice defense from their actual positions, so movement builds:
comfort in their spot
understanding of their area
confidence in what balls are "theirs"
We do not lecture areas —
we let movement teach space.
Front Row / Back Row (Simple Language)
Front row: players near the net
Back row: players near the end line
Defensive movement expectations are the same:
ready
move
get behind the ball
Positions (Location First)
Players learn positions as places on the court, not roles:
Position 1 — back right
Position 6 — back middle
Position 5 — back left
Position 4 — front left
Position 3 — front middle
Position 2 — front right
Movement is practiced from these spots, so players associate:
"This is my space."

### Page 7

Rotations (Minimal Exposure)
Rotation is introduced simply as:
"When we win the serve, everyone moves one spot."
Defensive movement patterns stay the same —
only the starting location changes.
HOW DEFENSE PROGRESSES OVER TIME
The movement skills never change.
The areas and speed increase.
We do not rush into defensive systems.
Early Phase (Week 1 Focus)
Goal: Learn movement and space
Teaching emphasis:
ready posture
stepping vs shuffling
staying in your area
popping the ball up
Primary cues:
"Ready"
"Read"
"Step / Step–Shuffle / Step–Shuffle–Shuffle"
"Platform"
"Up"

Goal: Faster movement and better area coverage
Teaching emphasis:
choosing the right movement skill
defending seams

Systems come later.
Non-Negotiables at All Levels
These never change:
Ready before the ball crosses
Feet move first
Use the smallest movement needed
Platform is built after movement
Legs lift the ball
Same Ready →  Read →  Move →  Platform →  Up sequence
Coaching Priorities to Remember
Defense is taught with the feet
Movement teaches space
Step before shuffle
Platform does not change
Teach terminology through repetition, not explanation
Cues are corrections, not explanations
