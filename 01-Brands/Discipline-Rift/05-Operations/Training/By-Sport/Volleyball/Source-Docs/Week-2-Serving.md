✅  ADD-ON OPTIONS (Block 2) — More retrieval
warm-ups that still support serving
These keep retrieval intent (touch rhythm + control) but give coaches choices.
Option A: Pepper with "Finish Freeze" (serving cue preview)
During pepper, on whistle everyone freezes in an "extended finish" shape for 2
seconds (arm up/forward).
Purpose: rehearses "freeze your finish" before serving begins.
Option B: Partner "Toss–Contact" Rhythm
Partners 1015 feet apart.
One holds ball still; other practices a gentle contact to partner and freezes
finish.
Swap every 5.
Purpose: contact confidence without net pressure.
Option C: "Target Point" retrieval
Coach calls a zone ("Zone 1!"). Players point instantly.
Then resume pepper.
Purpose: fast court mapping for the "serve to space" target layer.

### Page 9

Option A: Hula Hoop Serving Challenge (team scoring)
Setup: hoops spread on opposite court. Two teams alternate serves.
Scoring: inside hoop = 2; rim/touch = 1; in-bounds = 1 (if you want high success).
Coach focus: purposeful target + freeze finish.
Option B: Mission Impossible (serve →  sprint →  catch)
Setup: two teams. Each successful serve sends server to opposite side to sit and
become the catcher for the next serve.
Win: first team to cycle everyone through wins.
Why: high reps + fun movement + immediate consequence.
Option C: Join the Blob (targeting + engagement)
Setup: servers aim to land near or tag the "blob" area; successful "tag" joins the
blob on opposite side.
Why: keeps even hesitant servers motivated and repeats targeting.
Option D: Cops and Robbers (serve under time + movement)
Setup: "safe zone" hoop for serving; outside hoop = tag risk.
Goal: servers complete X serves in time; taggers try to catch them outside safe
zone.
Why: serving + speed + fun pressure.
Option E: Simon Says Serving (listening + technique)
Setup: teams lined up with balls. Coach gives commands; only some allow actual
serve.
Scoring: points only for correct "Simon" serves that land in.
Why: reinforces attention, routine, and self-control.
