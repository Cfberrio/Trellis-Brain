## Best-Fit Buyer
The best-fit buyer is a single decision maker responsible for a professional, date-bound event for up to 90 guests who wants the event to run smoothly and wants to avoid logistics embarrassment.

## Core Buyer Types
### Corporate planner / internal organizer
Usually an ops lead, HR coordinator, admin, or team lead organizing meetings, trainings, offsites, or presentations.

### Workshop host / trainer / creator
Usually someone running a paid session, educational event, hybrid workshop, livestream, or presentation where setup and production quality matter.

### Secondary buyer
A private-event host who still values compliance, convenience, and a controlled process more than a cheap blank room.

## What Their Day Feels Like
They are coordinating people, timing, seating, food, setup, and often A/V. If the event fails, they look disorganized. Their job is not just to book a room; it is to prevent the event from becoming a problem.

## Dream Outcome
"I want a professional space where everything just works, we start on time, the rules are clear, and I do not get blindsided by setup or A/V issues."

## Top Obstacles
1. Uncertainty around venue rules and penalties.
2. Setup and breakdown time pressure.
3. Uncertainty about technical support and A/V requirements.
4. Confusion about true total cost.
5. Fear of execution failure on the day of the event.
